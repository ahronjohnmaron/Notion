Guest Pass Page Lovable Prompt

This prompt is designed specifically for Lovable, which—unlike GPT—is focused on design, layout, and page structure, not copywriting. While GPT prompts help you create the words that sell, Lovable prompts help you bring those words to life visually. The prompt below tells Lovable to build a clean, professional landing page using your exact copy, without rewriting or changing the order of any text. It will automatically handle the layout, spacing, responsiveness, and placeholder visuals, so you end up with a complete, ready-to-preview landing page that reflects your message exactly as written.

You are a professional landing page designer and UX expert.

Design a responsive, visually engaging warm traffic Guest Pass / Booking Page using the exact copy I provide below.

Do not rewrite or rearrange any text — follow it line-by-line in the same order.

The goal is to create a seamless next-step page where users schedule their “interview” or “verbal confirmation” call to complete registration.

Consider this a branding guide:

[INSERT BRANDING INSTRUCTIONS*]

Design Instructions:

  • Use a clear, single-column layout for easy reading.
  • Make key lines like “WAIT! Your registration isn’t complete…” stand out with formatting or accent color.
  • Highlight any CTAs or booking buttons clearly (e.g. “Schedule your call”).
  • Include logical spacing between paragraphs for readability.
  • Keep the tone professional but friendly; design should feel trustworthy and action-oriented.
  • Include placeholder for embedded booking calendar.
  • Use neutral branding (fonts, colors, backgrounds) so clients can adjust later.
  • Maintain all emphasis (caps, quotes, bullets) exactly as written.

Structure:

Keep all sections in this exact order:

  1. Intro Warning Section: “WAIT! Your registration isn’t complete…” headline and short intro.
  2. Workshop Overview Section: Outline of Day 1 and Day 2.
  3. Participation Expectation Section: Commitment and seriousness copy.
  4. Booking Instructions Section: Explanation of “verbal confirmation” or “interview” call.
  5. Final CTA Section: Booking button or embedded calendar.

Output:

Generate a full responsive web page design with consistent formatting and placeholder visuals where needed.
