Registration No Interview

Our funnels are built around a pre-event interview during the registration process because it dramatically boosts attendance and provides other key benefits. However, if you're expecting a large influx of registrations or doing a paid event, scheduling individual interviews may not be necessary (in the paid event scenario) or feasible due to time or staffing constraints.

Thus, on the page after the event registration (or after they’ve paid), it’ll still be the “wait your registration isn’t complete” concept from the interview ☎️ Call Booking page, but instead of booking an interview, you’ll be asking them to book a feedback call.

But in this scenario, you won’t send them the meeting information (Zoom link) without booking their feedback call, so it’s important your copy says that. Thus tech-wise, you’ll want a feedback call calendar attached to an automation that emails them the welcome and Zoom info once they book their feedback call.

After booking the call, you can stay on the page or redirect to a call confirmed and here’s what to expect next.

Application

In this section, we’ll go over the details of the above, but adding the nuance of an application form to fill out for review in order to get approved for the event. So in this instance, they aren’t just automatically approved and require a little qualification.

Registration

This is the standard 🥶 Cold Landing Page Registration to Interview page (it should look exactly the same) except after opting in and providing their name, email, and phone, they proceed to the Application page instead of immediately to the feedback call booking page.

You will want to make a new optin form for this funnel. Now is also a good time to make the feedback call calendar. You’ll need one for a few days after your next live event and if you have an on demand version, you’ll want another one with a call booking window a few days from today.

Application

The Application Page asks the prospect some qualification questions because they are applying to see if they are compatible for attending your event. The reason we have an application page after the initial optin (instead of embedding the application on the registration page) is because we can have a follow up automation in case they don’t complete the application.

How that works on that backend if you’re using HighLevel is that once they optin on the landing page, they go into your CRM with an Application Customer Journey Stage (custom field) of Registered But Did Not Complete Application. If they did complete the application, their status changes to Registered and Completed Application. Thus, your automation will only send follow ups if their status is Registered But Did Not Complete Application. Here is a video walkthrough of how that works:

Application Received

After completing the application, they land on your Application Received page that essentially says we received your application, our team is reviewing, stay tuned for the results. You can also add testimonials or some other suggested action while you have their attention like follow you on social media.

As mentioned in the previous video, you have an automation that changes the status in your CRM from Registered But Did Not Complete Application to Registered and Completed Application and also notify the appropriate person that an application has been received for review.

Application Review

This can either be a manual process or you can automate it if you’re more advanced. Either way, you’ll want to have your automations set so that when they are approved (either manually or automated) by changing the status from Registered and Completed Application to Rejected or Approved and Have Not Booked Feedback Call and send an approved email and text message.

If they are not approved for some reason, you will want an option to trigger a rejection email based on status change of Rejected.

If they are approved, these messages will say congrats you’re approved and your next step is to go to the Feedback Call page to book your call.

Similar to the first step, you’ll want an automation to follow up on booking a feedback call if they don’t after a certain period of time similar to when they first registered and haven’t completed their application yet.

Feedback Call - On Demand / Live Event

On these pages, you’ll say congrats you’re approved and you’ll want a video to encourage them to book a feedback call.

That means you’ll want a calendar embedded with a booking window of within a few days after your event on your Feedback Call - Live Event page (or if you also have an on demand recorded videos of your event, it would be within a few days from today on your Feedback Call - On Demand page). Thus, be mindful that you’ll need to adjust this window after each of your events.

Once a feedback call is booked, your automation should then add them to your live event or on demand sequence.

Feedback Call Scheduled

After booking a feedback call, they should be redirected to your Feedback Call Scheduled page. This is where it says your “in” and watch your email/SMS for Zoom and event details.

Event Reset

On the day of your live event or maybe the next day, you’ll want to revise your system to accommodate the next live event date.
