Pre-event Interview Calls

backlinks

As part of the registration process for your event, the prospective attendee must have a quick 15-minute pre-event interview (Zoom) call with your interviewer. These interviews are the secret sauce for a high 90% showup rate at your event. And not only are they showing up, but they're showing up with their cameras on, excited to be part of your event, and ready to participate because your interviewer is properly filtering to have the right people at your event and setting the tone when doing these calls. Here's how all this works.

Setup

Before you do your interview calls, you want to make sure the following items are set up and ready because you'll need them during (or shortly after) each individual interview:

  • The Calendly booking page links for both days of the live event and the enrollment call per the Calendly setup instructions. (REMINDER: If you’re using GHL, you do not need to use Calendly.)
  • Event Tracker spreadsheet to take notes during the call.
  • You will also want the interview call script template revised for your business and ready to guide you through the call.

Qualifying Your Guest

Some people may want to make sure their guest is a more qualified prospect for their business before having them attend and interview or feedback call. For example, if you are servicing B2B and you don’t want any guest in your virtual event who works for a company that is not doing $1M or more in annual revenue, you can qualify them first by asking them to fill out a form.

If you’re dealing in B2C, some people may be sensitive to answering income questions (embarrassed, why should I tell you that’s my private info, etc.), so be aware that you may need to phrase the question differently or ask a different question that still gets you the info you need if you’re experiencing a drop in conversions meaning people not filling it out / not signing up for the call.

Example - instead of asking if they are employed, you can ask them do they have a job in addition to running their business (if that’s the situation of your target audience). And if they do have a job, what do they do? If they say cashier, you’ll know their income range without explicitly asking. Or if you’re dealing with B2B, you can ask about company size (how many people in the company gives you some idea of how much revenue the company makes).

Keep in mind these survey questions can also be done after they booked the call (versus being part of the call booking process). That way it won’t make it more difficult to register (unless you want to make your virtual event more selective to be a part of it). You’d execute this by configuring Calendly to redirect the user to your form page after the booking is successful.
