On Demand With Interview

Make sure to watch the training about creating your On Demand version! You can also find an example of SBM’s On Demand page here.

In the situation where your guest is not available to attend one of your upcoming live events, then you’ll want to direct them into your “on demand” funnel as a way for your guest to experience your event as best as possible through recorded videos that they can watch “on demand” anytime they want as if it was a Netflix series.

So on the interview, instead of getting them to commit to attending the live event on time with camera on, you’re getting them to commit to watching the videos and scheduling the feedback call for a date and time immediately after.

Recorded Event Videos

The first thing you’ll need to create is the videos for your event. You’ll want to break up your videos into chunks where there is one main golden nugget or idea/topic you’re discussing in that video and it’s not too long where it’s hard to digest, so something like 20-40 minutes each.

So if you recorded one long 3-hour video for day 1 of your event and another 3-hour long video for day 2 of your event, you’ll want to chop them up into multiple videos. At the start, just take the event where you felt like you did your best and use that. Over time, you may take one video from one event because you taught that topic particularly well that day and another video from another event.

On Demand Video Container

The next step is to load all those videos up into a place where your guest can consume them. If you have a password protected platform like Kajabi, you can put it there as a new product/course. If not, you can use a simple webpage, Craft doc, Notion doc, or something easy to get started.

The action items are designed to create engagement via text messaging with someone on your team (in this example it’s the “Community Manager”). More on this later.

Interview Changes

Now that your on demand videos are ready to be consumed, the next step is training your interviewer to modify their script to accommodate your new funnel.

During the interview, if the guest says they’re not available at the date and time of your event, your interviewer transitions to the following:

Okay, no prob! So we still have something available for you, but I just want to confirm the content is relevant to where you are and you would like to learn this content. Correct?

—- Guest responds —-

Okay, so what I could do for you is we have our on-demand training, which is the same content as the live event. It’s our end-to-end playbook on how to [YOUR EVENT TOPIC] and it’s 100% recorded video that you can watch anywhere at anytime. If I could share that with you, would you be committed to watching it all of the way through?

—- Guest responds —-

If they don't commit, here is how you should respond:

Hey, well, I don't want to share the training with you if you're not going to go through it. But if you are committed to going through it, I would be happy to share it with you.

If they do commit, here is how you should respond:

Okay. So, the videos are X hours long, which you can break up over a day or two or watch in one day if you’re really committed. When would you be able to watch all the videos and complete the playbook training?

—- Guest responds —-

Okay, great. What day are you thinking? By Wednesday? Probably like next Wednesday.

—- Guest responds —-

Okay, so for me to give you access to the training, I just need to be able to book a feedback call on the day after so we can get your feedback on how that experience was for you.

And you proceed to book the feedback call as you normally would.

Guest Access Email

After the feedback call has been booked, the next step is get your guest access to the on demand videos by sending them an email:

Subject: Access to our [EVENT TOPIC] video training

Hello!

This is your personal Community Manager here and I’d like to congratulate you for receiving access to our [EVENT TOPIC] playbook on demand video training!

[LINK TO VIDEOS]

Your guest pass access will expire on the day of your feedback call, so please be sure to set the appropriate time aside to engage in the training.

NAME :)

Community Manager Text Messaging

The last part of the process is to have your community manager (or whoever will be hand holding the guest through your videos) to text a welcome message similar to the email, but also asking them to respond with some confirmation that they were able to successfully access the videos.

This person, whether it’s you, your interviewer, your enroller, or someone else on your team, is responsible for:

  • Acknowledging the guest’s text messages to the action items for each video, cheering them on, and providing positive encouragement throughout the process.
  • Nudging them along to complete the videos in a timely manner.
  • Reminding them to attend the feedback call.

If you are paying someone to do this, you can’t really pay hourly because it’s a sporadic job with text messaging randomly throughout the day, so some options are giving them a fixed amount for the job or for each attended feedback call and/or enrollment.

💡
In our example here, we’re asking them to refer to an email we sent them because we’re using a unique link (see details at the bottom of the page) that can only be delivered via email and can’t be sent via text. If your videos are hosted on a regular webpage, feel free to insert the link into the text message too in addition to the email.

In terms of the tech, you can use something like Google Voice or some texting message web app (like ClickSend) that allows you to log in as well to monitor the conversations if you’re delegating this task.

⏳ Access Expiration

We’ll be explaining at some point how you can set an expiration date for access to the page that will be different for each person. This brings a sense of urgency for the guest to finish going through the videos because they’ll lose access on a particular date (day of the feedback call).

We’ll likely be doing this in HighLevel and it’ll take some time to thoroughly explain. If you’re an advanced HL user already, then here’s the outline: you can create an automation to add them into a course and remove access based on feedback call date (custom field in contact record). In that automation, the feedback call date is the event date as well and you can do countdown messages leading up to the expiration similar to how there are countdown messages leading up to a virtual event. If you have HL, this doesn’t make sense and you want to learn more now, attend a live tech call and we can explain further!
