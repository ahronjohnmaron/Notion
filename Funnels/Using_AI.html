Section 1: Identifying Your Ideal Audience

Understanding your ideal audience is the foundation of a successful event funnel. By clearly defining who they are and what they need, you can create tailored content that speaks directly to their desires and challenges. This section will help you dive deep into the demographics, psychographics, and emotional drivers of your audience.

1. Demographics: Who Are They?

Start by outlining the basic demographic information of your target audience. Consider factors such as:

  • • Age: What age range do they fall into?
  • • Gender: Are you targeting a specific gender?
  • • Location: Where do they live? Is it urban, suburban, or rural?
  • • Income Level: What is their typical income range?
  • • Occupation: What industries or job roles do they hold?

Prompt: “I am a [insert your role/how you help people: life coach/business coach etc] can you ask me 5 questions that will inform you on my demographic.”

2. Psychographics: What Do They Value?

Next, delve into the psychographics of your audience. This includes their values, interests, and lifestyles. Understanding these elements will help you connect with them on a deeper level.

  • • Values: What do they care about most in life?
  • • Interests: What hobbies or activities do they enjoy?
  • • Challenges: What struggles do they face in their personal or professional lives?

Prompt: “Please list the psychographics of my demographic”.

3. Dreams: What Are Their Aspirations?

Your audience has dreams and goals that motivate them to seek your coaching services. Understanding these aspirations allows you to position your offering as a solution.

Prompt: Use AI to brainstorm a list of common dreams and aspirations for your audience. What transformations do they seek, and how can your coaching help them achieve those goals?

4. Drains: What Holds Them Back?

Identifying the drains—those challenges or tasks your audience feels they must tackle—will help you understand the resistance they may have to pursuing their dreams.

Prompt: Generate insights using AI about the obstacles or burdens your audience perceives. What are the things they dread doing that prevent them from achieving their goals?

5. Doubts: What Are Their Fears?

Finally, recognizing your audience's doubts—the fears and limiting beliefs that may hold them back—will give you the opportunity to address these concerns in your messaging.

Prompt: Ask AI to explore the common doubts your audience might have about themselves or your coaching services. What fears might prevent them from seeking help or committing to change?

Conclusion

By the end of this section, you should have a comprehensive understanding of your ideal audience. This knowledge will empower you to craft messages and offers that resonate deeply, making it easier to attract and engage the right clients for your coaching business.

Section 2: Crafting Your Offer

In this section, you will learn how to structure your offer using a triangle model, where each side represents a pillar, and each pillar consists of three results-based milestones. At the center of the triangle, you will define the "big juicy result" your clients desire. By leveraging ChatGPT, you can streamline this process and create an irresistible coaching program.

1. Define the Triangle Structure

The triangle model is a powerful visual representation of your coaching offer. Each side of the triangle represents a key pillar, and the center highlights the ultimate transformation your clients can achieve.

Steps:

•
Identify Your Three Pillars:

"What are the three main areas of focus in my coaching program?"

Prompt Example:

"Help me brainstorm three key pillars for my coaching offer that align with my audience's needs."

•
Articulate the Big Juicy Result:

"What is the primary outcome that clients can achieve through my coaching program?"

Prompt Example:

"Generate a compelling statement that describes the big juicy result clients will attain after working with me."

Reminder:

You can ask ChatGPT for additional insights:

"What elements should I consider when defining my pillars to ensure they resonate with my target audience?"

2. Develop Each Pillar

Once you have identified your pillars, it’s important to define them clearly. Each pillar should represent a key aspect of the transformation you provide.

Steps:

•
Define Each Pillar:

"What does each pillar encompass, and how does it contribute to the overall coaching journey?"

Prompt Example:

"Describe what each of my three pillars will focus on in my coaching program for [specific audience]."

•
Create Engaging Names for Each Pillar:

"What catchy and memorable names can I give to each pillar to make them appealing?"

Prompt Example:

"Suggest creative names for my three pillars that will engage my target audience."

Reminder:

You can further enhance pillars by asking:

"How can I ensure my pillars are distinct and compelling for my audience?"

3. Establish Milestones for Each Pillar

Each pillar should include three specific, results-based milestones. These milestones represent actionable steps clients will take on their journey.

Steps:

•
Identify Milestones for Each Pillar:

"What three specific milestones can I create under each pillar to guide my clients?"

Prompt Example:

"List three results-based milestones for each of my pillars that will help clients progress toward the big juicy result. The milestones must be in the order in which we’ll achieve them.”

•
Define Outcomes for Each Milestone:

"What specific outcomes should clients achieve at each milestone?"

Prompt Example:

"Help me articulate the outcomes clients can expect to reach at each milestone for my coaching program."

Reminder:

Explore options by asking:

"What other milestones could I consider to ensure a comprehensive journey for my clients?"

4. Craft Compelling Language for Your Offer

The language you use to present your offer is crucial. It should clearly communicate the value and transformation clients can expect.

Steps:

•
Describe the Transformation:

"How can I articulate the transformation clients will undergo through my coaching program?"

Prompt Example:

"Generate a powerful description of the transformation clients will experience by engaging with my coaching program."

•
Highlight the Importance of Each Pillar and Milestone:

"What compelling language can I use to emphasize the significance of each pillar and milestone?"

Prompt Example:

"Help me create engaging descriptions for each pillar and its milestones that highlight their importance."

Reminder:

You can refine your messaging by asking:

"What words or phrases should I avoid to ensure my messaging resonates with my target audience?"

TOP TIP:

You can share the model scorecard with ChatGPT too:

Model Scorecard

Use everything here as a “scorecard” to see if your model is up to par because we want it to be as sharp and desirable as possible when you pitch it during your event.

✅ Do you have 3 pillars?

The pillars are the three sides to the triangle in your model. You can have four and make it a square model, but 3-5 should be the max. As an alternative to a triangle or square, some people have used a different object like a heart because it was a better fit for the theme of their brand or product. In the SBM example, the three pillars are Connect, Conversions, and Container.

✅ Are the 3 pillars in chronological order from left to right if applicable?

In our example, Connect is first (left side of triangle) because it represents traffic, Conversions is second (right side of triangle) because it represents converting that traffic to sales, and then Container is third (bottom of triangle) because it represents how to take care of the customer as the final step. If your model is topical (there is no sequence or chronological order), then this isn't applicable.

✅ Do the pillars have alliteration?

This is the least of importance when it comes to your pillars, but is a nice cherry on top if you can make it catchy with alliteration. In our example, Connect, Conversion, and Container all start with the letter c.

✅ Do you have 3 milestones for each pillar?

In the SBM example, the Connect pillar has the following 3 milestones: lead generation, lead volume, and lead nurture. You want your model to be balanced (each pillar having the same number of milestones) and thorough (your program looks complete) without being overwhelming (you don't want your customer to think "I have to do all that?"). Having too many milestones (beyond the 9) can also make it too time consuming and tedious when doing your pitch during the green, yellow, red game.

✅ Is your model using words that are short and punchy?

You don't want to use too many words (1-3 is good) for each pillar and milestone. You want to consolidate each pillar/milestone into as few words as possible so that it still encompasses what you want and still have impact (sometimes adding an adjective can be helpful). For example, a milestone you might have is "develop ways to execute your purpose" (6 words). You can trim that down to just Purpose (1 word). If you want to juice it up and add some alliteration, it can be Empowering Purpose (2 words). This not only simplifies it for your prospect, but also for you when it comes time to writing it out during your pitch.

✅ Is your model using language that is desirable or attractive?

If you're offering a program on finances, one of your pillars could be Budgeting. However, the word budgeting doesn't conjure up desirability because it sounds like "ugh that's not fun because it's something 1) dry that I have to do and 2) that tells me what I can't spend my money on." Instead, a phrase like Cashflow Mastery is stronger. Some more examples:

  • •Increasing Income → Income Acceleration or Income Multiplication
  • •Debts & Credit → Demolishing Debt & Amplifying Credit

✅ Are your pillars/milestones solving a big problem?

In B2B, Lead Generation and Conversion are examples of big problems that most people have. They're often good at rendering the service, but have trouble finding the customers and selling to them. Thus, these are big parts of the SBM model. If our model was centered around how to service and retain customers, it would be a very niche part of the overall market and not a big problem for our target audience.

✅ Is it something they would want to pay for?

This one is a bit similar to desirability, but comes with adding a money lens on top. An example would be Management versus Scaling. Management is the type of word that sounds like "I'm going to pay for your offer/service so that I can manage people and do more work?" versus Scaling "I'm going to pay to grow my business and get more customers!" It's a bit more subtle, but a great nuance to understand in making your model more attractive if you can use words that describe an outcome they want while minimizing the thought of having to work more (even though we know people will still need to put in the work after paying you). For example, when people are buying a self-help book, they mostly think about what outcome they'll get if the contents of the book are implemented, but they don't think about the effort required to read the book let alone apply the work described in the book. We don't want to unnecessarily shine too much light on the work required if we don't need to during the presentation of the model.

✅ Is your model using language that sounds expensive?

Since the price of your offer will be at least $2,500, you want your model to include pillars and milestones that sound expensive and not something they can go to Fiverr and get for $5. For example, if you help people with social media and part of your service is writing an Instagram profile, something like "Profile" as a milestone doesn't sound very expensive. Instead, you can call it "Social Brand Optimization" and it will sound like something worth paying a premium for.

✅ Is it something that is difficult to attain without you?

You want your model to include things that they wouldn't be able to do easily on their own (whether they do it themselves or there are dozens of other people or places they can go to) and need your coaching and support to obtain.

Clarity is a good example because it's hard to obtain by just searching the internet or reading a book. You usually need an expert like you to intervene and provide coaching and guidance to take someone out of their confusion and uncertainty in order to obtain Clarity.

Whereas Goal Setting is a lot easier for someone to attain by searching the internet, reading a book, or finding dozens of other coaches who can offer the same old goal setting.

However, if you do want to offer Goal Setting, we can instead change it to something like Goal Achievement, which is your network of people/clients/coaches who are there for your customer to provide support and accountability to make sure they take action and follow through on the goals you help them set. That's something they can't easily attain by themselves.

✅ Does your model create/include a desirable outcome?

Inside the triangle, you want to put a desirable outcome that your model/program will help them accomplish. In our SBM example, the outcome is a 7-figure business.
