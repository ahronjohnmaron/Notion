Desire to do something else

Ok so you’ve sat down and are ready to get to work. You’ve decided what you’re going to work on and then suddenly you have a thought come up that “oh I gotta do this other thing” and you get sidetracked and fall into a rabbit hole of different things to do. This could be wanting to check your text messages or email, check social media, wanting to get a snack even though you’re not hungry. It could be anything, including the desire to just not work because you don’t “feel like it.”

This desire to do something else is pulling you away. This force. This urge. For the sake of giving it a name, let’s call this FOMO. The Fear Of Missing Out on not doing this other thing is distracting you from doing this current thing. So what do you do about it? Here is a strategy on you can combat FOMO.

The skill set and habit you want to develop is the ability to be resilient to FOMO so that you can get back to what you’re doing by taking these steps:

Write it down and close the loop

Write down what that other thing is that came up. Example - buy paper towels. It’s important to be able to record it somewhere so that it exits your mind and it can close the loop on that thing knowing that it’s safely on paper or else it’ll keep thinking about it. If you write it down, it’s left your mind as a thing to remember to do and now it’s about dealing with the FOMO feeling.

FOMO is fleeting so breathe it away

Although FOMO can hit you hard, just know that it’s like any other feeling and it’s only temporary and it will go away. Because if you think about it, is it physically possible for you to non-stop feel this FOMO (or any emotion for that matter) forever? No. Whether it’s joy, sadness, anger - whatever the emotion, it’s fleeting and it will eventually go away. You just have to be able to go through it in the meantime.

So how long do you have to wait? 30 days?!? That’s ridiculous, right? Yep, I’m being silly because it’s impossible for an emotion to continuously run nonstop for 30 days. So how long does it really take?

The average emotion physiologically lasts 90 seconds in the body and brain.

That’s it!

Although it may feel insurmountable in that moment, just know that all you have to do is survive 90 seconds and you’re in the clear. Totally doable.

So how exactly do you do that?

Breathe.

Take a deep breath in, sit in however much FOMO you feel, and then take a deep breath out, while visualizing that FOMO leaving your body. Count that breath as 1.

Repeat.

Eventually, even if it takes 90 or 100 or 120 or 150 breaths, FOMO will dissipate, you will regain your power and control of your body, and you can resume what you were originally doing before you got distracted by the FOMO urge.

At the beginning, it might take 90 breaths, but over time, you will train yourself to shorten that time period. If you can, keep track of how many times a day this happens and how many breaths it takes each time for it to go away. Eventually it’ll be less and less in both categories as you get better at managing FOMO.

Get a breathing buddy

If you need support, phone a friend. Ideally you have an accountability buddy who is committed to helping you and breathing with you. (It’s good for health and longevity anyways.)

So the next time you get FOMO and could use some support getting yourself to breathe it away, call a friend and have them breathe and count with you on the phone. Over time, you’ll need to call them less as well.

And of course, reciprocate accountability and support if they’re going through a similar challenge.

😎 Fun side note: there is a really awesome app that is a support network of volunteers to help the blind called Be My Eyes.

Whenever they need visual help with something - say they’re at home in their kitchen standing in front of the pantry and don’t know which is the can of beans versus can of tomatoes - they press the button on the app and it basically round robin calls the volunteers and the first person who is available will pick up on a video call and they can be their eyes and tell them which can to grab. How cool is that?!
