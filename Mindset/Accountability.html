Accountability

Getting ourselves to take action despite not feeling like it can be one of the most challenging aspects of not just entrepreneurship, but also life.

But just because it can be difficult doesn’t mean we can’t stack the odds (using psychological strategies) to help you take action.

And that’s what this document is about.

Activation Energy

In terms of chemistry, activation energy is the minimum energy required to cause a process (such as a chemical reaction) to occur.

In terms of productivity, activation energy is the amount of energy it takes to get you to take that first step in taking action.

Often times the hardest part is getting started. Because we do get started, it’s not as bad to keep going.

For example, when you wake up in the morning, it takes a lot of activation energy to get out of your warm cozy bed to start the work day. However, once you’re up and you’ve washed your wash, it’s not that bad to stay out of bed. Getting out of bed (getting started) is typically the hardest part.

Thus, all the strategies here are mostly geared towards helping you get started and then support along the way until completion.

Being Clear on Your Champagne Moment and the First Step

Often times we have a goal of what we want to accomplish, but it’s unclear what the very next action is.

For example, you might want to hire a VA to help with your event marketing. That is the goal, but it’s hard to get started if you don’t think through and get clear on the very next action you have to do. It might be writing a job ad, it might be finding places you want to post the job, it might be asking around for a referral.

Break down a goal into actionable steps.

But what task do you choose?

A great way to think about it is Gary Keller’s question: “What's the one thing I can do such that by doing it everything else will be easier or unnecessary?”

A great way to answer that question is with something “big” enough that it’ll take you a week to accomplish. Demir & Carey Bentley, productivity experts, like to call it the “champagne moment” (CM) - something that if/when it happens, it’s worth popping a bottle of champagne to celebrate.

At first you might not be sure on what to choose as your CM, but start with something/anything and over time you’ll get better at discernment.

Deadline

How much time should you give to complete your task? Typically 2 days is enough time to complete it if it’s not a CM. But if it is a CM, make it a week.

Anything longer than that is not recommended because you’ll want to be able to course correct if necessary along the way week by week at the least.

And have an exact time as well.

I will complete ______ by Friday, January 1 at 5 PM Pacific.

Plan A - Place and Time

In order for you to execute, it helps a lot to have a designated place and time where you will do your thing.

Having the freedom of controlling your own time as an entrepreneur has its pros and cons. You have the freedom to do whatever you want, whenever you want, which can be a good thing overall, but a bad thing for productivity.

If you’ve ever had a 9-5 job, it should have been crystal clear where you had to show up for work and at what time. Your brain knows where to direct your body to do work. Otherwise, you get fired.

When it comes to your business, your odds of executing on your task increases if your brain knows exactly where and when to show up to do that task. For example, it could be in your den at 9 AM on Monday after you’ve dropped off the kids at school.

Plan B - Contingency

“Everyone has a plan until they get punched in the mouth.” - Mike Tyson

Now that you’ve got a Plan A on where and when to show up for work, you’ll eventually need to start incorporating a contingency plan when life gets in the way and punches you in the mouth.

For example, maybe your kid gets sick and can’t go to school and you need to take care of him at home, so 9 AM in your den goes out the window.

Understanding these things come up, your Plan B may be telling your partner to watch him when they get back from work after dinner from 7-8 PM so that you can execute on your task at that time instead.

Preparing to Execute

Preparation and doing the pre-work can help make it easier for you to execute when the time comes. For example, if your goal is to send 10 outgoing DM messages to invite people to your event, do you know what you are going to say and who those people are?

Doing the work ahead of time (finding the script template and modifying it for your business and identifying the 10 people you’re going to message) will make it a lot easier to execute come Monday 9 AM when doubt, fear, and a negative voice may creep in.

This is the same when it comes to exercising. It’s a lot easier to exercise in the morning if you sleep in your workout clothes for example so you can just roll out of bed and one less thing to do before going to the gym.

Penalty and Reward

As humans, we are motivated by reward (movement towards pleasure) and by penalties (avoiding pain), but are most powerfully moved by both at the same time.

Leverage this psychology by having some penalty and reward for completing your task.

For example, allow yourself to eat at your favorite restaurant if you hit your CM. And if you don’t, you owe $100 to a particular charity.

Your penalty should be high enough that it stings and annoys you that you rather do your task then receive/pay the penalty.

Desire to Save Face

Public embarrassment is something we all typically want to avoid. So if you put yourself out there in front of a group (such as during our accountability calls) announcing what you will accomplish by when, it makes it harder to fail because innately we don’t want to show up to the next call with mud on face and admit we didn’t do it.

Thus, have public accountability with a supportive group/network is a very powerful strategy to help you take action.

Support

Misery loves company (and supportive friends)! Sometimes the easiest way to get the activation energy you need to get started is by having a supportive buddy for accountability or to do it with you.

  • • You can phone a friend and say “hey, do you mind staying on the phone for a few seconds to help support me in getting started with ______.” They don’t have to talk. They just stay on the line.
  • • You can join an accountability call and announce in the group that “I’m about to start doing _____ and will report back at the end how much I’ve accomplished.”
  • • You can go on Focusmate and find someone in the world to co-work with you 24 hours a day 7 days a week.
