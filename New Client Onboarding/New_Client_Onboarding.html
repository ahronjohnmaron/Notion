New Client Onboarding

Ok so you have some clients who have signed up to your program and now it's about getting them onboarded!

Scheduling the First Onboarding Meeting

So when should you do your onboarding meeting for your new clients? Deciding on the date and time is the sweet spot combo of all these considerations (ideally less than 3 weeks after the event):

  1. ASAP to keep your new client's excitement and momentum and minimize buyer’s remorse. You want to get them going shortly after they've paid and are excited about your program. In an extreme example, your event ended on Tuesday and you had 1 attendee and sales call on Wednesday, so onboarding would start on Thursday the next day.
  2. Enough time after the event to allow your enrollers to get through all their sales calls. After the event is over, you'll need sufficient time for all your feedback/enrollment calls to happen, including rescheduled people and follow-ups. In an extreme example, let's say your event ended on Tuesday the 6th and you had 100 attendees. You'll probably need at least a week and a half to get through all the calls and onboarding can be as early as Monday the 19th. So for this factor/consideration, it's dependent on how many booked feedback calls and enrollers you have.
  3. A time that fits your schedule. Obviously, you'll want to do it on a date and time that works for you as an entrepreneur.
  4. A time that fits the majority of your new clients so that maximum amount of people show up. Keep in mind you have holidays, their time zones, and their general schedule (Saturday night is not good for onboarding because people will want to enjoy their weekend).
  5. If you have an evergreen program where you have open enrollment all the time, you'll want a set onboarding schedule. For us, it's always the second Thursday after the event that ends on Tuesday, which gives us about 10 days to get our feedback calls done.

Number of Onboarding Meetings

How much foundational material you have to properly kickstart your new clients will determine how many onboarding meetings you will have. For us, we like to make sure their first 30 days with us are strong, so we have 3 weekly onboarding meetings.

Notifying Your New Clients of the Date and Time for Onboarding (and Future Events)

Now that you have your date and time set for your first onboarding, let's sort out the different ways to let them know. Firstly though, you'll want to send them an onboarding email as soon as you can after taking payment. This can be sent manually/scheduled (through Gmail or email marketing service) or automated depending on your technical expertise. This email is the least that should be done, but ideally, you add in some additional calendar logistics:

  1. Calendar booking software. If you have a small handful of people, you may want to use your calendar booking service like Calendly and book them in for the onboarding call (like how the interviewers did for the event and feedback call). By creating an event in the calendar book service and manually booking the new client into that event, you can have the onboarding call added to their calendar and set up automated email and text reminders.
  2. Publishing a calendar of events. Using Airtable or Google Calendar (recommended), you can create a (Google) calendar that has your events in real-time for your clients to look at via a link (here's our Airtable calendar link as an example) and you can also create an iCalendar feed (with Google Calendar) that allows people to add your event calendar onto their own personal calendar as a layer. Most clients probably don't use this feature if they're not comfortable with tech, but it's a great option for those who are more advanced.
  3. Sending a calendar invite. The reason why Google Calendar is recommended over Airtable is because it can do the previous item mentioned above and you can copy paste a list of your clients into the Google Calendar event and Google will send them an automated email invite to add the event into their calendar. What's also great about this are the built-in notification features that Google Calendar has that allows you to send automated reminder calendar pop-up notifications and multiple emails (so you don't have to fuss with setting it up in your email marketing service).
  4. Sending text message reminders. You can use a pay-as-you-go text web app like ClickSend to schedule and send mass text messages to your clients reminding them of your upcoming onboarding, future events, or any other matter.
  5. Email. And of course scheduling emails to go out is the final piece. You can do this within your EMS like ConvertKit, you may be able to do it through the software that is hosting your hub (like Kajabi) because all your clients should be grouped in there automatically, or even scheduled through your normal email service like Gmail and bcc everyone.

Additional Zoom Account

At some point, someone else will be running or hosting your onboarding and/or client trainings and you’ll want to get a separate Zoom account. This is useful because you eventually don’t want these types of meetings under your own Zoom account as you won’t be able to run meetings concurrently.

For example, Adam has his own Zoom account for personal meetings and we have a team account under support@smartbusinessmastermind.com where all the virtual events and client training meetings are created under. This allows for our support person and Zoom host to start the meetings, add guest speakers, managing the admin, etc. without interfering with your personal account.

Preparing the Client Agreements

We recommend uploading your version of the Client Agreement into eSignatures.io because it's a great pay as you go service (~$0.50 per document with no monthly fee) that allows you to quickly and easily populate and send all client agreements via email for digital signature DURING the onboarding call. Here is a walkthrough video on how to use eSignatures.io. PandaDocs is another great option.

Why is it sent then for the first time to the client for review (together as a group during the call) and not sent to them sometime after you've taken payment and before the onboarding meeting? If you do that, you're more likely going to trigger questions and concerns when they have all the time in the world to themselves to go over it without you there. By going over it during the onboarding call, you have a chance to (re)sell your program and reinforce/remind them of all the great stuff they're getting. Also, you have the collective group there and an agenda to follow/continue on as a way to add a little encouragement to get the paperwork done as quickly as possible.

Conducting Your First Onboarding Meeting

Be sure to review the How to Create Evergreen Onboarding Enrollment Systems training in the hub to see how these onboardings are to be conducted, as well as the onboarding call agenda. Some things you'll want to cover at a minimum in your first onboarding call:

  1. Housekeeping - How the back office works, how to ask for support, training schedule, etc.
  2. Going over client agreements and getting them electronically signed

Adding Clients to Your Hub

After your onboarding call, you can now add your new client individually into your Kajabi hub or add in bulk. The reason we don't suggest doing it anytime before (even if they ask) is because we don't want them feeling overwhelmed and seeing so much content in there and not know how to navigate or where to start. If you'd like, you can give them some small homework assignment to help them keep the momentum and something to do until the first onboarding meeting.

Once they’re added, they should each automatically receive an email with their login credentials. You can also use Kajabi to send an email broadcast (versus using your EMS or a Gmail bcc) to all members from this point forward, such as following the onboarding call where you send them the recorded replay.
