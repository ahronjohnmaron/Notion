Pricing

Pricing - How much your program costs if paid in full or on a payment plan. Having your offer put together is also important so you can work backwards and craft a multi-day live event that is conducive for selling your offer during the Pitch.

How much should you price your program for?

The number you should focus on is your retail price 🏷️ This is the ceiling and the most that you will get from your client (because most people don’t see a price and offer to pay more, but would be awesome if they did!).

Since this is the highest they’ll pay, choose a number you’ll be happy with 😃 One strategy on how to set your retail price is based on the market price 💲 of your offer (what other people are charging for a service similar to yours) plus some premium ⬆️ So if other coaches in your space for example are charging $10k for a 6-month program similar to yours, perhaps make yours $12k.

For the most part though, it should be at least $5k. But what if my 30-day program now is only $1k? 😰 There are various things you can do to increase the value of your program to make it $5k, but one easy thing to do in this scenario is change it from a 1-month $1k program to 6-month for $6k. You’re just extending the duration of the program to increase the price.

But what if this number is too high for my prospects? 😰 Don’t worry about picking the perfect retail price (it just needs to be high enough to give you room to go down using scholarship discounts) because you’ll be using our money combination close and meeting each prospect where they’re at financially as you’re essentially giving each person custom pricing. We’ll talk more later about all this later (as it’s not uber important right now), but in a nutshell here’s how it works:

1.

During the pitch ⚾ portion of your event, you’ll tell them about your program and that it retails for $12k, but mention not to worry if it’s out of your price range because we have scholarships available that they can learn more about during the feedback call.

2.

When the feedback call ☎️ comes, you’ll be using the money combination 🔒 close to find out how much they can afford and are willing to pay and you give them a justified scholarship/discount/concession and create a price and pricing plan that works for them (and you). So if they say they love the program but can only afford $8k ($2k down and $1k/mo for 6 months) and you’re ok with that, then that’s ultimately the price for that person, which could be different for someone else.

Based on the above, you can see that the original retail price isn’t really that important to get “right/perfect” because it typically won’t be where you’ll end up at as it’s custom pricing for each person. It’s just a starting point to anchor some monetary value/price for your program to start the conversation and going from there with each person individually.
