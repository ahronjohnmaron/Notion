What is the Model?
This model is your visual blueprint designed to build an appetite and stir up a strong desire for your clients to pay you for the incredible results you’ll help them achieve. You’ll use this repeatedly on sales calls, your virtual workshops to “pitch”, and even as a guide to the milestones and success path you’ll be leading your clients on.

The model is built around one central, big, juicy result, supported by 3-5 pillars, with each pillar further broken down into three specific milestones or results.

You can get creative with the design using Canva, or draw this out on a piece of paper - do what suits you to get the creative juices flowing.

Why Is This Important?

When you pitch your high-value offer, your model is your ultimate tool for converting viewers into buyers.

It needs to be sharp, desirable, and, most importantly, easy for your prospects to grasp. This is what will set you apart and make your offer irresistible.

YOUR MODEL SCORECARD

Use this scorecard to ensure your model is up to par. Remember, the more of these elements you include, the stronger your model will be!

✅ Do you have 3-5 pillars?
These are the key areas your model will cover. Think of our three sides of a triangle. You can choose to have four (making it a square), but we recommend sticking with 3-5 pillars to keep it focused.

✅ Are the pillars in chronological order?
What is the order of success for your model? For example, if your process starts with generating traffic, then converting leads, and finally maintaining customers, your pillars should follow that flow.

✅ Do the pillars have alliteration?
This isn’t a must, but catchy, alliterative names (like “Connect, Convert, and Container”) can make your model more memorable.

✅ Do you have 3 milestones for each pillar?
Each pillar should have three clear, actionable milestones. This balances your model and makes it thorough without overwhelming your audience.

✅ Is your language short and punchy?
Keep your wording concise. Aim for 1-3 words per pillar and milestone. It should be powerful and easy to remember.

✅ Is your language desirable or attractive?
Use words that your audience will find appealing. Instead of something dry like “Budgeting,” try “Budgeting Mastery.” Make them WANT what you’re offering.

✅ Are your pillars/milestones solving a big problem?
Your model should tackle significant issues that your clients face. The bigger the problem, the more valuable your solution will appear. Make sure to stay away from vague words like,“ purpose” or “potential”.

✅ Is it something they would want to pay for?
Think about how your prospects will perceive your offer. It should sound like something worth investing in, not something they can achieve on their own, for example “enhanced nutritional game plan” vs “know how to eat healthy” - which would you be more inclined to pay for?

✅ Does your model use language that sounds expensive?
You’re offering a high-value service. Your model should reflect that by using terms that imply value. For example, “Improve Your Profile” could be elevated to “Social Brand Optimization.”

✅ Does your model create/include a desirable outcome?
At the center of your model, place the ultimate result your program delivers. This is the “why” behind your offer—the reason people will choose you.

Why do we suggest drawing this out during your pitch?!

Drawing this out makes it feel less rehearsed and more authentic. It’s also a great way of drawing the eyes of your attendees to where you want them to look instead of skipping ahead and missing out on the important information you’re sharing. However, some of our students have preferred using slides and that’s okay too! Just make sure if you use slides, to animate the words around your model so they appear upon your “click” and not remain all at once which can take away from the curiosity effect and learning experience. Choosing to use slides does make this exercise a little more challenging as it’s difficult to highlight the “red, yellow and green” which is why most clients choose to draw it out.
