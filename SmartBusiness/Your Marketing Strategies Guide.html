Your Marketing Strategies Guide

Welcome to the marketing strategy section—your roadmap to attracting the right audience and reaching your revenue goals. This guide is here to help you make your event stand out, fill seats, and convert attendees into clients, whether they’re part of your warm circle or brand-new prospects.

We’ve curated a diverse set of strategies, each suited to different strengths and comfort zones. Think of this as your marketing toolkit: from tapping into existing networks to expanding your reach through partnerships and live events, every strategy has a unique purpose.

All you have to do is select 3-5 that align best with your brand, resources, and comfort level.

In marketing, success often lies in your ability to pivot and adapt. Many strategies require ongoing adjustments based on what you see working—and what you don’t. This isn’t a sign that you’re off track; it’s an integral part of the process. Just as your audience is unique, so too are the approaches that will resonate with them. It’s important to note that marketing is a live experiment, not a static task. It involves testing, tweaking, and refining your tactics in real time.

Embracing flexibility allows you to spot patterns, make shifts quickly, and optimize for greater impact. For example, you may start with a DM outreach strategy, only to find that your audience responds more enthusiastically to email or a referral program. Or perhaps your hand-raising content on social media gains unexpected traction, signaling that this might be an area to invest more focus. Over time, as you gather more insights, you’ll get a stronger sense of what works best for your unique offer and audience.

This journey is also about building confidence in your approach. Address any doubts or skill gaps, and be open to refining your plan along the way. Over time, each adjustment brings you closer to understanding your audience and refining your approach, ultimately making each marketing move more powerful.

Here are the marketing strategies outlined for you, along with a checklist for each one to help you to stay on track.

1.
Warm 50 List

Summary: The goal of the warm 50 is to make a list of existing people in your circle of influence to invite to your event. They may not always be the ones to buy, but they can invite people who do buy from you. This is a simple and easy way to start if you don't have an existing audience and want to fill up your first event with people who already know you.

Your Checklist:

  • List of 50 contacts within the client's network (names and contact information)
  • Draft invitation message template: 📄 Intro to Contacting Your Warm 50
  • Event details to share with contacts (date, time, link, etc.)
  • List of potential incentives or offers for referrals
2.
Taprooting Referrals

Summary: The easiest people to invite to your event are the friends of the people who are already coming. This strategy allows you to grow your event at the speed of trust. You can host incentives, bring a friend contest, and other benefits to attendees who bring a friend.

Your Checklist:

  • List of confirmed attendees to reach out for referrals
  • Incentives for “bring a friend” (e.g., discounts, gifts, entries to a raffle)
  • Follow-up script or message template for attendees to encourage friend invites
  • Process for tracking referrals
3.
Client/Ambassador Referral Program

Summary: The most invested referral partner is the one who has already invested in you! Setting up a client referral program with a compensation plan and incentives is a fun way to reward your clients for referring others to your events.

Your Checklist:

  • Outline of the referral program (e.g., rewards, eligibility)
  • Existing clients/ambassadors list
  • Communication templates (emails, DMs, etc.)
  • System for tracking referrals and rewards
4.
Hand Raising Content & DM Scripts

Summary: These are people on your email list or social media who would benefit from your work but may not yet know what you do. This strategy allows you to package your hook, marketing promise, and a call-to-action to "call out" these people to come to your event!

Your Checklist:

  • Pre-prepared content using this template.
  • Call-to-action prompts for each post
  • Engagement tracking tool or spreadsheet
  • Clear description of the event and what attendees will gain
5.
DM Marketing

Summary: Our (25 x 12) process involves reaching out to 25 new people by 12pm each day. This strategy sends direct message (DM) invites daily, so you're proactively reaching out rather than waiting for people to come to you. This approach can eventually be scaled with virtual assistants or AI, mobilizing a cold audience to your events without requiring your direct involvement.

Your Checklist:

  • Daily goal and message templates (e.g., “25 x 12” strategy)
  • Contact list or criteria for new connections
  • System for tracking outreach and responses
  • Responses, objection handling, and follow-up templates
  • Consider Social Sales Pro/Flow Chat
6.
Contest Marketing

Summary: This strategy involves giving away a free offer publicly on your social media in exchange for engagement and participation in a contest. Rules typically include calls-to-action like "Comment below" or "Tag a friend" to win a prize. This generates waves of new leads, which can become new clients or event attendees.

Your Checklist:

  • Defined contest rules, prize(s), and eligibility
  • Social media graphics and post templates
  • List of requirements for participants (e.g., tag friends, share, etc.)
  • Method for tracking entries and selecting winners
7.
Speak and Sweep (Live Streams)

Summary: Going live allows transparent interaction with your audience, showcasing what you do. This strategy includes following up with live stream attendees and sparking conversations that lead to your events.

Your Checklist:

  • Scheduled live stream topics and calendar
  • Pre-written scripts or outlines for each live stream
  • Follow-up message templates for live attendees
  • Engagement tracking sheet to log viewers and engagement
8.
LinkedIn Events

Summary: Set up recurring events on LinkedIn to generate RSVPs and leverage LinkedIn’s features for increased organic reach and conversion.

Your Checklist:

  • LinkedIn event creation template and instructions
    More information on our LinkedIn Automation here
  • Recurring event schedule and frequency
  • Pre-written invitation and reminder messages
  • RSVP tracking and follow-up message system
9.
Email Marketing

Summary: Whether you have a small or large email list, we’ll show you how to write in a way that consistently converts subscribers into event attendees. This is a copywriting masterclass in connecting and converting.

Your Checklist:

  • Contact list segmented for targeted campaigns
  • Pre-written email sequences (invitation, reminders etc)
  • Email tracking and analytics setup
  • Outline of event highlights to entice RSVPs
10.
Podcast Prospecting to Land Dream Clients

Summary: Many dream clients won’t respond to typical DMs or emails. In this strategy, you create a live stream show or podcast to interview these prospects. As a thank-you, you offer a free "guest pass" or "discovery/strategy" call, creating a bridge for potential clients and collaboration opportunities.

Your Checklist:

  • List of potential guest clients to interview
  • Guest invitation templates (email or DM)
  • Event guest pass or discovery call offer details
  • Follow-up plan after the interview
11.
Smart Business JV Summit

Summary: Imagine having guest speakers who add value by creating workshops tailored to your attendees and promoting your event to their audience. This strategy accelerates event registrations and leverages guest influence.

Your Checklist:

  • List of potential guest speakers or collaborators
  • Partnership outreach templates
  • Presentation guidelines for guest speakers
  • Co-promotion agreements and tracking
12.
Speaking Engagements

Summary: Paid speaking engagements and "Speak to Sell" opportunities are powerful ways to grow your brand and bring clients into your programs. This strategy covers both types, providing a playbook for each.

Your Checklist:

  • Defined target events or audiences for speaking
  • Speaking proposal templates (fee-paid or speak-to-sell)
  • Follow-up templates for audience members
  • Engagement and lead tracking tools
13.
Affiliate/JV Marketing

Summary: Create a list of "influencers/gatekeepers" with your audience and pitch them to promote your event. You’ll pay a commission for any clients they refer, turning top influencers into powerful promoters.

Your Checklist:

  • Top 100 influencer or partner list
  • Affiliate proposal and pitch templates
  • Terms of partnership (e.g., commission rates)
  • System for tracking affiliate leads and conversions
14.
Building a Marketing Team

Summary: Hire people for 1-2 hours a day to promote your events. This includes finding, training, and managing a team to maximize reach.

Your Checklist:

  • Clear job descriptions for team roles
  • Training materials and schedules
  • Team communication plan and task tracking system
  • Method for monitoring team performance and outcomes
15.
Company Partnerships

Summary: Partner with tools or platforms in your industry for joint collaborations, leveraging each other's databases for a win-win promotional strategy.

Your Checklist:

  • List of aligned companies and contact details
  • Partnership proposal templates
  • Outline of mutual benefits and promotion plans
  • System for tracking partnership outcomes
16.
Utilize YouTube for Your Events

Summary: Use YouTube to attract your ideal audience with targeted videos, optimized for search, and direct them to register for your event.

Your Checklist:

  • Choose one core topic and commit to creating content around it for the next 6 months
  • Use the provided GPT prompt to craft searchable, click-worthy titles and descriptions in your brand voice
  • Include your event as part of your call to action when it’s relevant
  • Post consistently (weekly) to build momentum and trust
  • Repurpose long-form content into Shorts or clips to reach more of your ideal audience
17.
Instagram Marketing Strategies

Summary: Kristen Duke shared how to transform Instagram into a business growth magnet by using bite-sized emotional storytelling, strategic hooks, and story-to-DM flows that build trust and fill your events.

Your Checklist:

  • Pick 3–5 core story themes you’ll share consistently
  • Post a Story with a strong hook that names a pain point
  • Add a CTA for viewers to DM/comment a keyword (use ManyChat if desired)
  • Reply in the DMs with value first, then transition to your invite
  • Mix Stories for private conversations and Posts for public visibility
  • Show up 2–3x per week with authentic, emotional storytelling

18.
Cold Email

Summary: Cold email is like cold calling, but via email — you’re reaching out to people who don’t know you yet. This strategy involves sending personalized emails to a verified list of your target audience using specialized tools like cold email software or AI-CRMs. Whether you're using basic outreach tools or advanced automation with support from an integration specialist, the goal is to scale visibility and invite the right prospects to your event.

Your Checklist:

  • Acquire triple-verified email list of your target audience
  • Set up dedicated sending domains and warm up email accounts
  • Connect to cold email sending software or AI-CRM
  • Integrate tracking for engagement and conversions
  • Modify and personalize pre-written cold email templates and sequences
  • Send campaigns and monitor inbox replies
