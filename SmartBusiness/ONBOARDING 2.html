PART 1: EVENT AGENDA

Your event agenda is your blueprint for delivering a transformative experience. This table outlines the flow of your event, helping you plan each segment with precision.

Why the Event Agenda Matters

The event agenda is your strategic plan to:

  • •Welcome and Engage: Kick off your event by creating a warm, inviting atmosphere that encourages attendees to feel connected and valued.
  • •Differentiate Your Event: Stand out from typical webinars by offering unique value and actionable insights that your attendees can’t find elsewhere.
  • •Build Credibility: Share your personal backstory and client success stories to connect with your audience and establish trust.
  • •Encourage Participation: Use interactive exercises and discussions to make your content relatable and applicable to each attendee’s personal challenges.
  • •Structure with Purpose: Ensure your event flows smoothly with a balanced mix of teaching, activities, and breaks, all leading up to your offer presentation.
  • •Create an Impact: Your ultimate goal is to leave attendees feeling inspired, motivated, and ready to take action (stepping into your program) based on the strategies you share, with some real results too!

1.

Headline: Capture Their Attention
Your headline is the first thing visitors will see, so make it count. It should immediately address the main problem your audience is trying to solve. This approach ensures your message hits home with those who need it most.

•

We have a GPT prompt you can use to create your headline:

🔺
Event Headline GPT Prompt
2.

Sub-headline: Set the Stage
In this section, briefly introduce your event. Highlight the unique value and insights you’ll be sharing. You can lean on the high-impact content you’ve planned in your event agenda. Emphasize what makes your event different from a generic webinar—what new perspectives or strategies will attendees gain?

Remember, including the event date and time is optional. If you’re concerned that a specific date might deter sign-ups, you can leave this detail out - we do! The focus should be on the transformation attendees will experience.

3.

What Will You Learn?: Highlight Key Takeaways
Use your event agenda as a roadmap for this section. Outline the key takeaways in a way that’s concise and compelling. What specific strategies, insights, or knowledge will attendees walk away with? Focus on the transformation—how will attending this event change their current situation? Make this section as outcome-focused as possible, drawing directly from the tangible and unique content you’ve structured in your agenda.

4.

Plus Statement: Add Extra Value
The "Plus" section of your template is where you can really make your event stand out. Think of something powerful that adds extra value—a surprising bonus or an exclusive piece of content that your audience can’t get anywhere else. For example, "Plus, I’ll even show you how to implement these strategies in just 30 minutes a day." This statement should feel like a valuable addition that enhances the overall offer, enticing your audience to sign up. Make it pack a punch!

5.

What Will You Do?: Engage with Actions
What will attendees be able to do after they’ve attended your event? Here, you can slightly paraphrase what you’ve written in the "What Will You Learn?" section, but focus more on the actions your audience will be equipped to take. Don’t overthink these two sections - it’s fine to have some repetition!
