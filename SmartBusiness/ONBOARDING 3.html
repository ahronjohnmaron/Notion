PART 1: CREATE YOUR HAND RAISING POST WITH YOUR BOLD PROMISE

Your bold promise is the core of your marketing message—it’s what you’re “selling” over and over again. You may have multiple bold promises for different audience segments, and your goal is to test different promises to see which one resonates best.

Here are some examples of a "hand raising post" with a bold promise:

Example 1:

🤖 I’ve uncovered the latest AI strategy that helped my client generate an extra $10K this month—without a single sales call. I have 4 free spots this week to show you how we did it.

Note: This is a free demo (no payments allowed).

Comment "AI demo" and I’ll send you the booking link.

Example 2:

❤️ I created something I call the "Strict 7" and my client used it to attract her dream partner—the man she truly wanted, not the one she used to settle for. I have 2 free sessions available this week to help you create your own 'Strict 7'.

Note: This is for women who are actively dating and ready for lasting love.

Comment "Love" and I’ll send you the booking link.

How To Write Your Bold Promise:

To help you craft yours, start by listing 3–5 unique things you do to get your clients results that your clients find both desirable and different.

Template:

I just discovered a powerful way to help my clients [insert result] by [insert your unique method or approach]. I'm looking for [# of people] who want to experience this/see how this works— for free.

Note: [insert clarifier, such as eligibility or what to expect on the call]

Comment “[insert trigger word]” and I’ll send you the link to book.

Checklist for Your Bold Promise:

  • ✅ Does it present something unique?
  • ✅ Is the outcome highly desirable?
  • ✅ Does it solve a real, urgent problem?
  • ✅ Is it clearly for your ideal audience?
  • ✅ Did you remove friction with a no-pressure offer?
  • ✅ Is your CTA easy to follow (ex: “Comment ‘freedom’”)?

Tips to create your hand raising post:

•
Focus on a Tangible, Desirable Result

Your promise should speak to something your audience already wants—not what you think they need.

→ Example: “…who want to add $5k/month” or “…get into their dream relationship faster”

•
Use Non-Perfect Numbers: Saying you're looking for “4” or “7” people (instead of 5 or 10) to make it feel more real and exclusive.

→ Example: “..I’m looking for 7 online coaches who want to test this—for free.”

•
Keep Your CTA Short and Simple: Keep your CTA easy to follow—choose a word that is easy to type and aligns with your message.

→ Example: “GROWTH” or “ME” instead of “ENTREPRENEUR”

Problem-to-Solution Content

If you want to truly connect with your audience, speak directly to their pains and problems.

If you want people to pay you, show them the solution you've discovered that bridges the gap.

This style of messaging is called Problem-to-Solution Content.

When you’re focused on injecting cash quickly, post this type of content daily.

Test different pain points and solutions to discover what resonates most.

PART 2: CRAFTING YOUR DM SCRIPTS

Your DM scripts are a direct way to engage with your audience and guide them towards your event or offer. You don't wait for people to come to you. You go to your audience. We call this 25x12. Send out 25 messages by 12pm inviting them into your offer. The template provided will help you structure these scripts, and here are some examples and additional tips to ensure they are effective:

FOR THE EVENT:

Hey [name],

I hope you're doing great! I wanted to reach out and invite you to an online workshop specifically for [Insert Target Audience], who want [x result].

Based on you being [Insert Target], getting some feedback from someone like you would be extremely helpful to me (and in exchange you’d learn how we help clients "insert y result").

Would you be interested in joining our online workshop and sharing your insights?

Warm Regards,

-[Your Name]

Warm Regards,

-Amy

TIPS FOR THIS STRATEGY:

When does this script work?

This message only works if you open up with a question that they are already motivated to answer, doesn’t require any thought to respond, and takes only 1-3 words to get back to your message.

1.
Start with an Ice Breaker:

Use a multiple-choice question where only one of two answers can be given. This removes the need for excessive thought and makes it easier for the recipient to respond.

2.
Don’t Build a Friendship in the DM:

People will feel like you only became their friend to invite them to your workshop and will often feel swayed by your intrigue into their life. It’s better to be direct with your intention up front.

3.
Avoid Getting Too Personal:

Be mindful of the nature of your questions. Avoid overly personal inquiries that might make the recipient uncomfortable.

For example, if you’re a budget coach, you don’t want to ask a personal question like this: “How are you managing debt in the current economic climate?”

This is intrusive, requires too much thought, and requires a lengthy response to get back to you.

By following these guidelines, you’ll be well-prepared to create a compelling hand raising post and effective DM scripts that resonate with your target audience. Once you’ve completed your drafts, submit them to the appropriate reviewers (your assigned coach) in a Telegram direct message.
