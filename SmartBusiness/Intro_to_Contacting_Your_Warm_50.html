Intro to Contacting Your Warm 50

When you’re just starting out, you may not have a polished brand, big-name speakers, or huge demand for your event yet. And that’s okay because what you do have is often far more powerful: intimacy, care, and connection.

The first group of people you want to reach out to is your Warm 50 – those closest to you who already know you, believe in you, and want to see you win. These aren’t cold leads or strangers. They’re the people who will pick up the phone, respond to your text, and show up because it’s you asking.

This stage is not about selling. It’s about:

  • Care — showing them you value the relationship enough to personally invite them.
  • Commitment — creating a sense of shared accountability where they feel part of something meaningful.
  • Inspiring Action — letting your passion spark their curiosity and motivate them into action.

Your leverage here is not logos, hype, or social proof. Your leverage is the intimacy of a personal invite. When someone feels you truly thought of them specifically — not just a mass message — they’re much more likely to commit.

When you reach out to your Warm 50, keep these principles in mind:

  1. Lead with relationship. Check in, ask what they’ve been up to, and reconnect first.
  2. Share from the heart. Position your event as something that matters to you personally, and invite them to be part of it.
  3. Make it about them too. Let them know why you thought of them and what value they’ll walk away with.
  4. Ask for commitment. Not in a pushy way, but with sincerity — “It would mean a lot to me to have you there.”
  5. Inspire action. Cast vision that they’re not just attending an event; they’re part of a something great you’re starting.

Here’s What Not To Do (Example Text)

Text Example:
Hey [Name], hope your day is going well! I was thinking about you. I have an event coming up on “How to Be the Happiest You!” It’s going to be so juicy with goodies, and I couldn’t help but think of you! You will absolutely love what I’m going to cover, and so I immediately thought of you. It’s going to be small, quaint, and on Zoom with a touch of aligned inspiration. I just can’t wait to get your feedback. It’s on September 30th at 2 PM and ends at 5 PM PST. Would love to have you join us. Let me know.

Levels of Relationship

  • People Closest to You: Close friends / close acquaintances.
  • Past Clients: Previous 1-on-1 clients, group, or membership programs you ran in the past.
  • Acquaintances: Friends in your network (who know you), people who follow you on social, people you’ve met in networking groups, or people in your social circle you haven’t connected with in a while.
  • A Familiar Face: Someone you’ve met once or twice. You have them saved in your phone or connected on social media. You “know of each other” but wouldn’t consider them a close friend or acquaintance.

Memory Jogger:

1. People Closest to You (Close Friends / Close Acquaintances)

Think of people you have a personal connection with and interact with regularly.

  • Best friends from childhood, high school, or college
  • Your closest family members (siblings, cousins, parents, adult children)
  • Friends you see at church or faith groups
  • Gym buddies or workout partners
  • Neighbors you know well
  • Friends you go on trips or vacations with
  • Your spouse’s or partner’s close friends
  • Parents of your kids’ closest friends
  • Members of your weekly small group or accountability group
  • Roommates (past or present)
  • Friends you confide in during tough times
  • Friends who always show up at your parties, barbecues, or get-togethers
  • Longtime mentors or advisors
  • Colleagues you still keep in touch with outside of work
  • Friends you celebrate birthdays or holidays with regularly

How to Reach Out:

Method of Contact: Phone (pick up the phone and call them).

Phone Script:

  • Rapport: Hey, what’s going on [Name]? (Small talk)
  • Pattern Interrupt: Hey, real quick—what are you doing on [date of event]? Are you by chance free at [time of event] that day?
  • Share the Event: Okay great! The reason I'm asking is that I’m hosting an intimate workshop on Zoom, and I’ll be sharing on [topic]. I was wondering if you’d be open to sitting in on the event. You can join right from your phone. It would mean a lot to have you in the room so I can practice the content I’m teaching and get your feedback. Would you be open to sitting in with us?
  • Seed Referrals: If there’s anyone who is [insert ideal target audience] that you’d want to invite, let me know. I can share a guest pass with you for them. If they want to attend, I’ll give you the guest pass link, and they’d just jump on a quick call with me to go over the event details so they can have the best experience. It would be really nice if you could bring someone with you, as I really want to make an impact with this event. Sound good?

[Note: If they have influence/following]
One thing I was wondering—if I were to share a post, would you be open to sharing it on your social? It would mean a lot, as I really want to impact people through this event, and I can give you a couple of guest passes for people to join us. Would you be open to that?

Housekeeping:
Okay, great. Just one last thing—I’m asking everyone to have their camera on as it creates better energy in the room, and I’m asking that you try to stay for the whole event. If you can do that, it would be great, as I want to keep the vibe of the room high. Also, if you want to throw some love in the chat, I’d love that—we can always use the extra hype. I’ll also be mentioning my program during the event for people who want to take next steps.

Ask for Feedback:
Cool—after the event, I’d love to get your feedback and hear your thoughts if that’s cool with you.

Close (Tie Down):
Right on! I’ll text you the link. Also, send me your calendar email so I can send you the invite there. You’ll probably also get some reminder texts leading up to the event. Thanks again for your support—I’m grateful for you.

Change Subject:
So what are you up to the rest of the day?

2. Past Clients (1-on-1, Group, or Membership Programs)

Think of people who have invested in your services before or been part of your programs.

  • 1-on-1 coaching clients (current or former)
  • Group program participants
  • People who bought a past course, workshop, or challenge
  • Membership site or subscription program members
  • Clients who purchased from a bundle, summit, or collaboration
  • People you’ve done consulting projects for
  • Clients you’ve freelanced for
  • People who joined a past beta program or pilot offer
  • Attendees of your past paid webinars or events
  • Private clients you’ve worked with via referrals
  • Corporate or organizational clients
  • Individuals you gave strategy calls to
  • Clients who’ve left testimonials for you
  • Clients who “ghosted” or stopped mid-program
  • Clients who asked for refunds or paused services (still part of your history)
  • Repeat buyers who’ve invested more than once
  • Clients who you supported in collaborations or partnerships

How to Reach Out:

Method of Contact: Text then Phone

Text:

3. Acquaintances (Friends in Your Network)

People who know you, but you don’t have a super close relationship with.

  • People you met at networking events or conferences
  • Friends-of-friends you see occasionally
  • People you interact with on social media who regularly comment or like your posts
  • Local community members you’ve crossed paths with (volunteers, organizers, PTA, etc.)
  • Former coworkers or teammates from past jobs
  • People in mastermind groups you’ve joined
  • Members of Facebook groups, LinkedIn groups, or online communities you’re part of
  • People you’ve collaborated with lightly (podcasts, panels, guest workshops)
  • People you exchange DMs with occasionally
  • Friends you only see once or twice a year (holiday parties, weddings, reunions)
  • Extended family you don’t see regularly (cousins, in-laws, distant relatives)
  • Parents of your kids’ classmates (school drop-off line connections)
  • People you’ve sat near at church or other gatherings
  • Neighbors you wave to but don’t hang out with often
  • People from hobby groups (sports leagues, art classes, book clubs)
  • Your old teachers, coaches, or professors
  • People from community service or nonprofit work you’ve done

4. A Familiar Face (Met Once or Twice / Connected but Not Close)

These are people who are on the edge of your network — you “know of” each other.

  • People you’ve been introduced to once at an event
  • People you’ve met briefly at weddings or parties
  • Social media followers you’ve connected with but don’t know personally
  • People you swapped numbers with but never followed up
  • Vendors you’ve hired once (photographer, hairstylist, handyman, etc.)
  • People who reached out about your services but never became a client
  • Acquaintances from the gym, yoga class, or fitness groups
  • People you’ve met through volunteer events once
  • Baristas, waiters, or store owners you’ve chatted with before
  • People in alumni networks you’ve exchanged messages with
  • Realtors, insurance agents, or financial reps you’ve interacted with once
  • People you’ve carpooled with (kids’ sports, school events)
  • Someone you’ve met at a community fair or market
  • People you’ve sat next to on a plane or at a conference session
  • People in your extended online network (friends of friends on Facebook, LinkedIn, Instagram)
  • Speakers you’ve met at events
  • People you’ve exchanged business cards with
  • Neighbors you’ve introduced yourself to but never really talked with

How to Reach Out:

Method of Contact: Text, Phone Call #1 → Text, Phone Call #2

Text or DM (Goal: Ask a question they’re excited to answer):

  • “Hey [Name], when we met at [event/place], you mentioned [thing they said]. Something crossed my path that made me think of you. Mind if I give you a quick call to share?”
  • “Hey [Name], it’s been a while since we last connected and talked about [topic]. Something crossed my path that made me think of you. Mind if I give you a quick call to share?”

Phone Call 1:

  • Rapport: Hey, what’s going on [Name]? (Small talk, immediately share what made you think of them).
  • Pattern Interrupt: Hey, so tell me, how have you been since we last talked about [topic]?
  • Listen: Catch up and build connection.

Important: Ask questions around the event but DO NOT mention the event. Goal = identify a need.
Affirm the connection and Edify them where you find opportunity.
Exit the call. I have an idea I want to share with you…but I got to run. I'll shoot you a message when I get a moment later on this week.
