Notes on Effective DM Marketing:

Bad Messaging:

  • Asking a question that causes someone to have to think about the answer.
  • Asking a question that the prospect is not motivated to answer.
  • Asking questions that require vulnerability to answer “are you living in your most authentic self”
  • Leaving out, or offering a weak, bold promise.

An example of messaging that likely won’t work:

“Hey name, I run a mastermind training that is all about how to unlock your authenticity so you can feel good about yourself/feel aligned in your own body.”

  • Why won't it work? Not everyone is ready for this, would understand it, is clear on what that would mean, or believe that it is worth putting aside time to learn about it.

Good Messaging:

  • Is it something your target audience can relate to?
  • Is there a clear ROI in your bold promise?
  • Is the bold promise highly desirable?
  • Is the promise worth putting life on hold for two days?
  • Is the bold promise something they already want?
  • Real Estate Example: “Are you taking on more listings right now?”

Principles of Effective DM Strategy:

  • Ask a question someone is motivated to answer. For example, when targeting an entrepreneur: “I was wondering if you were taking on new clients right now?”

    We then leverage their answer as the reason to offer help.

  • Build the motivation, or desire, and then ask the question.
    • For example: “Hey NAME, I am running a mastermind training that is all about how to unlock your authenticity to feel totally aligned so you can reach more of your goals without the fear of what others think. After reviewing your profile I wanted to reach out because I’m hand selecting people that I would love to have sit in on our experience. It’s free and you can’t buy anything during this training. Would you consider sitting in with us?”
    • This uses the bold promise to build desire and ask the question.
  • Earn trust, follow up, and stay consistent. Follow up every 2-3 days with kind, thoughtful messages, such as: “Hey just circling back, not sure if you saw this. Wanted to bump this up on your feed.”

    Engage with their content in between messages. Then continue follow up: “Hey just circling back, I know things can get busy.”

    (Showing grace, consistency, thoughtfulness - all attributes that build trust, which is why the prospect is more inclined to respond after 3-5 follow ups)

When things aren’t working…

If you’re doing all this and not getting results, it could be due to your profile. Does your content relate to what you talk about in your DMs? If not, this could lack credibility.
