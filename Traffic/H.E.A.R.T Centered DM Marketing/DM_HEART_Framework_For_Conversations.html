The H.E.A.R.T Framework for Conversations

1. Honor their Response – Acknowledge what they shared with warmth and care.

If they're doing well / excited about something:

  • Example: "That sounds amazing! I love hearing that you're in a good place right now!"
  • Or: "That sounds really tough. I can imagine that must be challenging for you."

2. Empathize & Relate – If appropriate, connect with their experience or express understanding.

  • Example: "I’ve been there too, and I know how that can feel. You’re not alone!"
  • Or: "That’s such a beautiful season to be in—so much growth happens when we slow down and reflect."

3. Ask a Thoughtful Question – Encourage them to open up more.

  • Example: "What’s been the best part about that for you?"
  • Or: "How have you been navigating that?"

4. Reinforce with Encouragement – Speak life into them.

  • Example: "You’re doing better than you think, and I know you’re meant for even greater things!"
  • Or: "I love your resilience—it’s inspiring!"

5. Transition with Purpose – If relevant, transition to something meaningful, whether it's an invitation, resource, or simple encouragement.

  • Example: "If you ever want support in that area, I’d love to share something that might help!"
  • Or: "Keep me posted on how things unfold for you—I’d love to hear!"

  • "You’re definitely not alone in this. I’ve had seasons like that too, and they always stretched me in unexpected ways."

3. Ask a Thoughtful Question – Encourage them to open up more.

If they’re doing well / excited about something:

  • "What’s been the most rewarding part of that for you?"
  • "What’s been the best part about that for you?"
  • "What do you think has contributed most to this season of growth?"
  • "What’s next for you now that you’ve reached this milestone?"
  • "You’re a light, and the world needs exactly what you have to offer!"

If they’re facing challenges / struggling:

  • "What has been the hardest part of this for you?"
  • "How have you been navigating that?"
  • "If you could receive support in any way right now, what would be most helpful?"
  • "What do you feel you need most in this season?"
  • "Is there anything you’ve realized about yourself through this experience?"

If they’re feeling uncertain / stuck:

  • "What’s been on your heart the most about this?"
  • "If clarity wasn’t an issue, what would you ideally want to do next?"
  • "What’s one small step that feels doable right now?"
  • "What’s something you feel called to explore more deeply?"
  • "If you knew things would work out in your favor, what decision would you make?"
