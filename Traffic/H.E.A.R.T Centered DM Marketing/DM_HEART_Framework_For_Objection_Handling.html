The H.E.A.R.T Framework for Objection Handling

1. Honor their Response – Acknowledge what they shared with warmth and care.

  • Example: Thanks for being open to share.
  • Thanks for getting back to me.

2. Empathize & Relate – If appropriate, connect with their experience or express understanding.

  • Example: “I can only imagine how you feel with so much on your plate.I appreciate you sharing that as managing many moving parts can certainly be overwhelming, but I’m believing something powerful will unfold for you in this season”
  • "I’ve been there too, and I know how that can feel. You’re not alone!"
  • "I have had so many people try and sell me too.I don’t blame you for guarding your time and space as I often respond the same way to these kinds of messages from people I’m not familiar with.
  • I can totally understand why you wouldn’t be interested and I don’t blame you for guarding your time and space as I often respond the same way to these kinds of messages from people I’m not familiar with.
  • I can totally understand why you wouldn’t be interested and I don’t blame you for guarding your time and space as I often respond the same way to these kinds of messages.
  • "I have had so many people try and sell me too so I completely understand why you would not be interested”
  • "I have had so many people try and sell me too so I completely understand why you would believe there is a catch.”
  • “I know with travel coming up it can be a lot to take on anything new at the moment.
  • “I love that for you as so much growth can happen with the right mentorship.”
  • “I know what it's like to constantly be sold things especially when finances are tight.”
  • “I know what it's like to have a lot going on”.

3. Ask a Thoughtful Question (If you sense a need to build stronger connection) – OR Present a New Possibility and Re-Ask the Question. (If you want to re-position the opportunity to book a call)

  • Example: “If I were to share that this is an invite only experience and you can not buy anything during the call/workshop...would this shift your interest in possibly joining us/booking a chat together?”
  • Or: What if I told you that you can’t buy anything during our time together, and that we even get to do a 1-on-1 just to make sure this workshop is in alignment with your goals?
  • Here’s an idea..what if I were to send you a recording so you can watch on the plane or when you are in a relaxed place…
  • “I fully acknowledge that you are in another program but what if I told you that you can’t buy anything during our time together, and that we even get to do a 1-on-1 just to make sure this is something that could add to what you are already doing?
  • Here’s an idea…what if I sent you the recording so you can watch it in the after hours of the day so it doesn’t distract you from what’s most important to you?
  • Let me know if this is something you’d be open to…If so, would it be okay if I send over the link?
  • Knowing this would you be open if I sent over the invite?
  • Or: "Let me know if this is a possibility and if I could send over more info about what we would cover in our time together?
  • Since this is more about support not sales. Would you be open to a quick chat to make sure this aligns with your goals?
  • Since this is more about support not sales…would it be okay if I send over more details?
