Podcast Interview Strategy

Make sure to check out the training that goes along with this!

Overview of the Process

The goal is to start a show that you can use as a prospecting tool to connect with your ideal clients. Interviews are known as “The Great Equalizer” because they allow you to have neutral influence for whoever gets a chance to be on your show. You want to pick a show title that is motivating for your dream clients to be on. For example: If they are an entrepreneur, you may title your show: “Top Entrepreneurs of Today”. This way, the guest is more motivated to saying “Yes” to your show since it’s edifying to them.

Note: If you are in a psychographic such as “Relationship Coaching” or “Trauma/Healing Work”, you may not be interviewing prospective clients but rather, you may be using your show to interview influential people who would be ideal promotional partners to your offers. This podcast strategy can get you in the door by “Networking UP” to gain access to those who would normally not be open to a connection if it wasn’t for the opportunity to be interviewed.

What’s In it for the podcast guest?

  1. (Can Enroll New Clients)They can repurpose the interview to their own audience which leads back to their offers.
  2. (Brand Authority) The interview positions them as an authority to their audience.
  3. (Saves them time)This saves them time from having to make their own content and make their own promotional assets.
  4. (New Potential Leads) Even if you have a small community or following this could lead to potential leads or clients from your audience.
  5. (Networking with you) You have a solution that can enhance their life or income through productive partnership opportunities you present.)

What’s the Order of Success?

The Foundation to this Strategy

Before getting started you want to come up with a “show name” based on the outcome you want. For example, if you want to use your podcast to target dream clients, you will come up with a show name that your guest would align with the kind of show your guest would say, “yes” to.

For example: If you are targeting high-level entrepreneurs, you may have a show called: “The Elite Entrepreneur”. This episode title would be edifying to your guest and help validate the way the way they see themselves.

Another example: If you run a relationship coaching program, and you want to target influencers that have your audience for a JV promotion, then you may call it, “Influential Relationships Podcast.” On this show your focus is on interviewing influential people that have “Your audience”. Let’s say they are a “Financial Coach”….their topic may be about financial aspects of a relationship. This way it’s beneficial for the coach and beneficial for you as they can be an ideal JV partner!

Definitions to know:

  • JV partner = Joint venture. (You promote something of theirs and they promote something of yours.
  • Promotional partner: (One person promotes the other)
  • Paid partner: You pay them to promote
  • Integrated partner: They integrate you into their business model for ongoing promotions.

Step 3: Running Your Pre-Interview (The Script)

You’ll setup and jump on a pre-interview before the actual interview: (This is typically a few days or weeks before the actual interview so you can gather information that will be helpful in supporting them and also in preparing you to offer your discovery or strategy call) Remember, you goal is to gain them as client, not necessarily get clients from the subscribers who are listening, although this can be a result that comes later as your influence grows.

What Do You Say on the Pre-Interview?

One of the first things to do when you get on Zoom is to acknowledge the elephant in the room - Why are you reaching out to me? Are you trying to sell me something? This usually comes up in the form of a question - How did you find me or what made you reach out?

One of the things that I look for when I interview somebody is somebody who's doing amazing things in the world and I like to spotlight them on my platform to be able to help them get their message out. And what's in that for me is that I get to bring value to my audience because they don't always want to hear from me all the time they want to be able to hear from amazing people like yourself and you might get some clients or followers as well, so it's a win-win. And when I see the right person who could be a fit, I like to invite them onto my show, so that's why we're here.

Alternatively, you can bring it up first by starting the interview with something like:

Before we begin, I just want you to know that there isn't anything weird that goes on here and I'm not going to sell you to be on my show and you don't have to send this to your email list or promote it. A lot of people want to though because it puts you in an authority position when your audience sees you being interviewed. But the main objective here and what's in it for me is that I get to bring value to my community by spotlighting and interviewing someone who has a great message because my audience doesn't always want to hear from me all the time and it's nice to have perspectives from other experts like you. And during this process you might get some clients or followers as well, so it's a win-win. Does that sound good to you?

At this point, their response should be positive and grateful and you'll know you've set up the call in the right direction.

The next step in the process is understanding their business and offer because this valuable information for you to assess later how we can best possibly help and collaborate. The beautiful thing is that this information can be obtained as part of an important process of putting together a successful interview. Here is how the script goes for this next portion:

Ok great, so what I'd like to do now is create a framework with you for the interview. We want to give value to our audience on a topic that you love to speak about, while at the same time do it in a way that best sets you up to lead our audience to a product or service that you have to sell. We can do this by working backwards. I'd like to understand what you're selling and reverse engineer from there... Cool? Okay great! So…. (The objective is to ask the questions that help you find a need. For example, because I’m a business coach, I ask questions that help me find a need. Here are some of the questions I would ask below:
  • What's the name of your offer, program, product, or service?
  • What is included in that program? What do customers get? How long is it?
  • What is the price for that?
  • How long have you had this program running for?
  • How many students do you have currently in your program?

Based on the last two questions, you can do the math and get an idea of how much revenue they're making which can help you tailor your presentation.

What if you are not in the business niche? Let’s say you help people with their emotions…

You may ask questions such as:

  • What has been the biggest personal growth nugget in your life that has helped you manage your emotions?
  • What else has helped you manage your emotions in business?
  • Where have you been challenged with boundaries, and what boundaries have you had to set?
  • What areas regarding boundaries, life balance, that you still find to be a struggle that you want to grow in next?

Although these are pre-questions you are asking to lead a good interview, you are also “Finding a NEED” that will allow you to offer a discover call to solve the problem they mentioned they are still struggling with. So be strategic in your question asking as it’s not only good for the show but helps you identify their problems you can solve.

Identifying the Bridge

The next question to ask is critical for identifying the "bridge" that you'll be promoting:

  • What have you been currently offering for free as a way to get leads. For example do you have a lead magnet or a website you want to share?

The purpose of this question is to dig and see what thing you can promote during the interview that will bridge your audience into the influencer's ecosystem and eventual program. What we do NOT want to do is promote the program directly because we don't want your interviews to feel like a pitch fest and burn your audience from doing a bunch of these interviews. A great bridge is typically something like a free lead magnet - PDF, ebook, webinar, etc. You can set this up with the following:

Ok great. What freebie would you like to promote on the interview? Is there a particular domain they can go to? Or would you rather have them connect with you via dm to your IG?

Crafting the Interview Content

Now that they've agreed to promoting that bridge, the next step is to continue working backwards and develop the key topic you'll talk about for the interview:

  • So what is the main message that we could talk about that you think would be a good segue into your freebie?

Asking this question will help you come up with not just the topic to discuss for the interview, but also the hook and title for it. So for example, if the bridge is free quiz on identifying their emotional intelligence for business, then the topic of the interview can be on emotional intelligence for entrepreneurs and the title can by something catchy like "How to Turn Your EQ to Profits in Your Business." Once you have the topic identified, you proceed with the core of the content:

  • What are 3 bullet points that we can cover?

You can brainstorm a handful of topics, but be sure to narrow it down to three because it keeps it short and sweet for the interview.

Also keep in mind that while you're doing this, remember to make sure the content fits your brand and values. Don't agree to a topic or bullet point that you wouldn't feel comfortable sharing on your social media. Thus, during the brainstorming session, approve and veto the content in your head, but of course elegantly guide the discussion towards content that works for you and your audience.

  • Okay great…what do you think we should call this episode?

Introducing the Influencer

We're now at the end of constructing the interview, which is helping them put together the beginning - a proper influencer introduction to start the interview. Most people don't have good bios and introductions, so you'll likely need to lead them through this as well, which is another value add and level of professionalism they get to receive from you.

  • What are a couple of key highlights about your background?
  • Since you have your XYZ program, I like to add into your introduction that you're the creator and founder of XYZ program that helps people do _________.
  • Who are the people you help? And what result do you get them?

Interview Logistics

You now should be all set with the content for your interview. Next step is sorting out the logistics for the interview itself like the date and time. You will want to book the interview as soon as possible because the longer it gets drawn out, the lower the chances of it happening.

  • Ok great so now all that's left really is scheduling the date and time for the interview. Based on what we talked about today, this interview is going to be incredible and my audience would love to hear this information from you sooner than later. How does your schedule look like over the next few days?
  • Have you ever gone live on IG before? Here is how you join my live IG broadcast.
  • And what's your mobile phone number? Just so that I can text or call you as a reminder or if there is an emergency.
  • What's your email address too? (if not collected from the initial invite on your Calendly)

Telling Them About You

Up to this point, it's all about them and not you. And that's how it should be. Sometimes the interviewee will try to get to know you in the beginning, but what you want to do is redirect it back to them as quickly as possible:

  • Hey, I'd love to share, but I'm so curious to know about you right now. Tell me about...

But at this point of the pre-interview call, you're ready to tell them about you:

  • Ok great we're all booked for the interview, but before we jump off, do you mind if I just share a couple minutes about myself so that you know who's interviewing you?

That is a critical question to memorize and deliver. Then from there you can tell them a little about you and what you do, but be sure to transition into this next part. You can have this on your screen to reference, but do NOT read from it:

So I help people, “Insert Your Bold Promise” and I have been doing this for, “Insert credibility” and have helped, “Share example of transformation”. “Share what makes you so unique and different”. So that’s why I do..Pretty cool, right?

The Offer:

I really like who you are, what you're up to, and I love the message you have to share. As a thank you for being on my show, I'd love to gift you a free strategy call/discovery call and we can go over, “Insert Juicy Topic Relative to them”. It would be an honor to gift you that, would you be open to doing that call together?

If the energy is not there, you can seed the mastermind training for after the interview.

I can't make any promises, but maybe one of these days I can gift you a free strategy call/discovery call and we can go over, “Insert Juicy Topic Relative to them”. Just as a gift to you for being on my show. Just an idea if you'd be interested in that, I can maybe set that up in the future.

The key part of this is just planting the seed. You're not going to have a conversation about this any further (you'll do that during the post-interview call), but it's a good sign if they want to talk more or are interested.

Post-interview Call Logistics

The final and one of the most important steps for this pre-interview call is to sort out the logistics to talk immediately after the interview for the Post-interview Call because that is where you will be inviting them to our mastermind or strategy call.

Okay great…So we're all set on the date and time for the interview. It's probably going to be about 20 minutes and then right after it's over, I'll call you on your phone so that we can do a quick recap and make sure I can support you with any additional mentions or resources we may have talked about during our interview. It'll probably be a quick 5-minute chat. Sound good?

Step 4: The Interview

Immediately after the pre-interview call, you'll want to send out a calendar invite so they have the interview blocked out in their calendar. Better yet, if you're using a service like Calendly, you can create an event with an email and text reminder workflow for automated reminders, though you should manually text confirm in the morning of the interview as well.

When it comes time for the interview, here are the instructions again on how to join your live IG broadcast. You may even want to include this and any other additional tips you have in your Calendly automated email reminders.

Follow this Framework for Your Interviews:

Introduction:

Welcome your audience with enthusiasm and set the tone for the interview. Briefly introduce yourself, your platform, and what the audience can expect from today’s session. This should be concise but engaging to hook viewers in right away.

Example: "Hey everyone! Welcome to today's Instagram Live! I’m [Your Name], and I’m so excited for this conversation because we’re diving into [briefly mention topic]. You definitely don’t want to miss what’s coming!"

Bring on Guest & Guest Introduction:

Invite your guest to join the Live and introduce them. Provide a short, exciting bio that highlights their expertise, achievements, and why they’re the perfect person for this topic. Make sure to emphasize why your audience will benefit from hearing their insights.

Example: "Let’s welcome [Guest Name] to the show! [Guest Name] is a [guest’s profession or expertise], known for [specific achievement or expertise]. We’re so lucky to have them here to share their insights on [topic]."

Share Topic:

Clearly state the topic of the interview. Explain what the conversation will cover and why it’s important to your audience. Frame it in a way that builds anticipation and curiosity.

Example: "Today we’re talking all about [topic], and how it impacts [audience's interest]. This conversation will give you [specific takeaways], so you can [benefit]."

Cover 3-5 Bullet Points:

Dive into the main content of the interview. Discuss 3 to 5 key points or questions related to the topic. Give your guest space to share their expertise, and feel free to interject with your own thoughts to keep the conversation dynamic.

Example bullet points:

  1. "What inspired you to get into [field/topic]?"
  2. "What are some common misconceptions about [topic]?"
  3. "How can people implement [specific strategy/tip] in their daily lives?"
  4. "What’s the biggest challenge you’ve faced in [related area] and how did you overcome it?"
  5. "What are your top 3 tips for anyone struggling with [issue related to topic]?"

Ask any Additional Questions:

If the conversation is flowing, feel free to ask follow-up questions that dive deeper into any interesting points the guest raises. This keeps the discussion natural and engaging, and can lead to unexpected insights.

Example: "That’s really interesting! Can you elaborate on how [specific strategy] has impacted your approach?"

Close out with Interviewee CTA (Freebie):

Wrap up the main discussion and give your guest a chance to promote something, whether it’s a freebie, a course, or a social media handle. Let your audience know how they can stay connected and access valuable resources from the guest.

Example: "Before we wrap up, [Guest Name] has a special freebie for all of you! [Explain freebie or promo]. You can grab it by [share link or instructions]."

Thank Interviewee and Guest for Being on the Show:

Express gratitude to the guest for sharing their insights and thank your audience for tuning in. Keep this short but sincere.

Example: "Thank you so much, [Guest Name], for being here and sharing all your wisdom! And thank you to everyone who tuned in today—it was a fantastic session!"

Close with a Powerful Quote:

End the Live with an impactful quote that resonates with the topic. This leaves the audience with something meaningful to reflect on.

Example: "I’ll leave you all with this: '[Insert powerful quote]'—something to keep in mind as you move forward. Thanks again for watching!"

This structure will help ensure your Instagram Live interview runs smoothly, remains engaging, and provides clear value to your audience.

Step 5: Post-interview Call

Immediately after the interview, call the influencer on the phone and have the following conversation:

  • Oh my gosh, that was amazing. Thank you so much for being on my show. Your message was so powerful. I really loved _______ about our interview.

Now is the time to book the discovery/strategy call

  • If you don’t remember on our last chat, we discussed me giving you a free discovery/strategy call on “x topic”. Did you still want to book that?

If yes, book it and on the call follow the 7-step sales framework to lead into your offer!

OR (JV partnership angle)

  • Hey it was so great interviewing you! I think there is so much synergy and cross over with our audiences based on what you do. What are your thoughts on doing a promotion together? I think it would be fun to make some money together and I can make all of the assets so you don’t have to do any work other than send an email. Is that something you’d be open to?
