Voice Chat Marketing

Watch the Voice Chat Marketing Training
  • Event page is only for cold traffic where you need a longer webpage to describe the event, such as paid ads, JV affiliate mail out, email solo ads, or a link in your IG profile.
  • If you're sending warmer traffic where you've described the event and explained there's an interview requirement, you send them to the calendar booking page. This type of traffic includes people already on your email list or from DM marketing.
  • Even if you know the person really well like a current client, you still want them to go through the interview call.
    "I'd love for you to come to our event. I'm going to have you go through our traditional registration process because I want you to experience what that's like so you can have the best experience so I'm going to have you go through that anyways. When doing the interview, I just want to get you set up for the event so we can go over the culture of the event because we want it to stay at a high level so I'm just going to ask you a few quick questions. One of the things that we'd love because we're friends is a testimonial at the end getting your feedback, so do you mind if we book that call now so I'll give you a link so you can join us?"

    Note for Friends/Acquaintances: People who are a close friend or think they might not do business with you, they may be pleasantly surprised after going through your live event and want to be a part of it. Since they are a close friend, when it comes to pricing, you can give them a discount: "Because we've been friends for a while, I want to do something awesome for you. Instead paying full price, why don't I let you in for this amount because of our relationship."

    Alternative Approach: Or the opposite. "Because we're friends, you're going to pay the full amount because I want you to respect the room and honor the process." Otherwise, they may not value the experience.

  • If using Calendly, there's a snippet of code (will be provided by SBM) that you need to add to remove the call title so that it's just the calendar.
  • Using Zapier or a similar tool, you can automate email and text follow-up for those who registered but didn't book the call.
  • One of the important skillsets you'll need, especially when doing cold DM marketing, is reframing negative mindsets, perspectives, and objections from cold prospects.
  • Understand that when people have these types of negative responses, they're referencing their past experiences.
  • Objection Example: How could you invite prospects into your paid mastermind? It's a disservice to your clients.
    Reframe and respond:

    "Hey [NAME]. Thank you so much for sharing your perspective on our mastermind and I absolutely agree it should be sacred and that's why we have a pre-interview process that we do have you go through with our community manager because we want our room to stay high level with the right people. That's actually one of the reasons why we're reaching out to you because I can see you're a high level person yourself and you're the kind of person we'd like to invite you into the master. Now why would we want you to sit in on our clients? Well, the reason why is because we have a culture of generosity. A lot of our mastermind students have been able to become a part of it because they were also guests at one point and that's part of how we give back to the community. Here's the cool thing - you can't even buy anything on the mastermind so you truly get to walk away with the experience and our hope with that is you would get started soon or some point in the future, but that's totally up to you. So would you be open to giving it a shot and jumping in and joining us for this mastermind?"

  • Objection Example: I don't appreciate the cold outreach. It's inauthentic. You don't know me.
    Reframe and respond:

    "Hey [NAME]. Thank you so much for your message and so I appreciate you sharing that with me. I totally understand where you're coming from because there are so many weird things that people get pitched in their DMs and a lot of times they're just not real opportunities. I want to let you know that I actually hand selected you to sit in on my mastermind with our paying clients because we want our room to stay high level and I think you're high level and I love the impact you're making because I can see on your page that you've really done the work so I'd love to invite you to sit in on our community and just share with you how we [TITLE OF YOUR EVENT / CONTENT TOPIC] and if this content seems like something you'd like to learn, it'd be an honor to have you as my guest."

  • Objection Example: The interview process is too complicated to go through. The calendar thing.
    Reframe 1:

    "We want the room to be high level. We don't just want anyone to register. Ever been on a webinar where everyone's camera is off and people are coming in and out of the room? It's distracting. We do interviews to make sure they're serious about coming to our events."

    Reframe 2:

    "Hey [NAME]. Totally understand how you feel that way. We understand that there are a lot of people who have been burned in different programs and things like that so we're very sensitive to who we let in. That's actually why we reached out to you because I see you as being very high level and you seem like you have a great heart, really sweet, sharp, have a great message so wanted to invite you into our community because I think you're the kind of person who would really do well in our group, so would that be something you'd be open to sitting in on?"

  • Edify them on their higher version of themselves. As a simplistic example, if you want someone to be friendly towards you, you'd tell them "Oh you seem friendly" or "I appreciate how friendly you are." You're making a container of a higher version of themselves for them to step into.
  • Often times people are afraid of making the decisions to get to the next level and they need a third party (a leader like you) to challenge them, show them past their limitations and into their possibilities, and guide them.
  • Train so that objections don't rattle you. Their objection is their trauma response. Don't let that trigger you and become your trauma response.
  • Have conviction that you will give all the tools and support that people need to be successful, but don't expect everyone to be successful or else you will be disappointed because not everyone will be successful as there are too many variables you can't control. But do know that they will be better because of you. So market like you're running for the President of the US.
  • Have milestones on the journey you want to take your customers through, but be flexible on how you get there because they dictate what they need. Don't be rigid on your trainings, but instead listen to the questions and issues your community is having and make your trainings around that.
