The DM Outreach SOP

Welcome to your guide on mastering DM marketing to consistently hit $10k+, $20k, and $30k months.

You can leverage direct outreach to fill your programs, sell your offers, and scale your business — without solely relying on attraction marketing or paid ads.

Many entrepreneurs get stuck waiting for their content to generate leads organically, but the reality is that unless you already have a large, engaged audience, you need a proactive approach.

This is where DM marketing becomes a game-changer.

Before DM engagement

Branding:

  1. Before anyone responds to a DM they typically check to see who the DM is coming from. For that reason, you want to make sure your profile appearance is aligned with the promise your are making. For example, if you are promising you help people, “Breakthough Fear”, then you should have content, quotes, and a brand theme that shows you do this kind of work!

Note: This doesn’t have to take months, this can take a couple of weeks.

Here are a few steps to take:

  1. Post twice a day temporarily to establish brand presence:
  2. Choose a template or theme using Canva, so your colors, text, and thumbnails look congruent! Remember “Aesthetics” help boost influence.
  3. Add your “bold” promise in your bio!
  4. Add a high resolution photo of you in your profile picture: (Make sure you are smiling)
  5. You need social proof or engagement: If no one is liking your content, that can feel like you may be a “Fake” account. So you can build your own “engagement” pod. This is where you recruit a small circle of friends that work to support each others content each time you post. You can ask others in the group to join your pod, if you don’t know who else to ask.

THE Winning DM Mindset! [25x12]

The goal is to send 25 messages by 12pm. This is a process we call 25x12! (If you are not free to start your day with DMs, then do it in the time block you have set).

Must Understand: Hardly anyone responds to the first message. They respond to the 4th and 5th follow up messages. For example, “Hey name, just circling back to see if you got my last message”. Sending 4-5 of these with 2-3 days in between each message is the key to success in the DM’s.

Out of 25 messages, you should expect to be in conversation with 1 person. So at the end of 5 days, you are in talks with 5 people! This over 1 month will compound and be 20 conversations. This should lead to 4-5 calls booked and 1-2 new clients!

If you notice, this number will increase over time as you will be able to compound these efforts in following months as you stay consistent.

We are wanting 3-4% to engage which means that over 95% of people will not respond to you or may even be upset you are sending them a cold DM. This is why you want to make sure you are not focused on the ones who say “no” but rather give your time to those who say “yes”.

Va’s (Virtual Assistants & Software)

The goal is to eventually document your process and objections and have a va set appointments for you! We call this having an “appointment setter”. These VA’s often work overseas in the Phillipines or in Pakistan and charge $5/hr to do this task for you so you can stay focus on sales. As you get a successful setter, then you can move up to hiring multiple who have you booked to the ideal amount of call you want to take! If you are looking for a VA who can help you with this task, we recommend you do it for at least 3-4 weeks so you can understand your target and the role you will be delegating. Always find a VA who already has experience DM’ing!

Another tool that can help accelerate your inbound lead flow is “Software”. Today there are softwares that can generate leads for you. Here in SBM we have two softwares we have found to help accelerate our appointments setting. One is on LinkedIN and the other is on Instagram. (We have not found a software to help with Facebook as of now). You can watch these trainings in separate videos. The ultimate goal is to have softwares running, and VA’s to manage the inbound conversations while they are still doing their own outbound outreach!

Where do you start?

It’s important to understand that each platform has it’s way of DM’ing that important for you to know!

Instagram Steps:

  • Step one: Build a list of target profiles. You may do this by finding the person your audience follows and look at their audience. You can also search key #hashtags that are likely to be used by your target audience.
  • Step two: For each profile you want to engage with, you want to first follow, comment on 3-4 posts, then wait for the follow back. Then send your 1st message so it can land in the main inbox! This means you will need to nurture for the first week before you can expect to send your first messages. We call this “The cooking period”.

Parameters: Do NOT send more than 20-30 messages a day on the instagram platform to keep your account in good standing.

Facebook Steps:

It’s important to understand that people on Facebook will not see your message until you are first a friend with them. For that reason, you’ll want to follow the steps below.

  • Step 1: Build a list. You can use facebook groups to find your ideal target audience. Request access to 5-10 of your favorite facebook groups.
  • Step 2: Begin sending friend request. You only want to send 25-30 friend request a day to be in good standing with your account. For the first 60 days, make sure you are not sending more than 10 request per hour. (If you have not engaged with this activity, sending more can flag your account).
  • Step 3: When you send a request on Facebook, make sure to include your message with it. As soon as they accept your request, your message will land in their main inbox!
  • Step 4: Send 4-5 follow up messages to engage them with 2-3 days in between each message.
  • Step 5: Book appointments, make sales, and delegate this task to a VA!

LinkedIN: (For LinkedIN, we recommend hiring a VA or using the software we recommend here at SBM. This platform is better to be leveraged as it requires more VOLUME to find success.

The Script

You can use this script, alter it to align with your messaging, and start bringing in leads RIGHT NOW:

Hey name,

I hope you're doing great! I wanted to reach out and invite you to a free “Coaching Call/Strategy Call” specifically for "Insert Target Audience", who want "x result".

Based on you being "Insert Target", getting some feedback from someone like you would be extremely helpful to me (and in exchange you’d learn how we help clients "insert y result").

Would you be interested in experiencing coaching in this area and sharing your insights?

Warm Regards,

-Your Name

When they’re interested in the content, the next step is to invite them to call by setting the time and date. But before you do, it's expected you're going to get some drop-off when the rubber meets the road going from I'm interested to take action.

Follow Up:

Remember, the majority of your converstions will begin after the 4th and 5th follow up. You can send a message like this:

Hey NAME, I know how busy the inbox gets. Just want to bump this to the top of your feed. 🙂

OR here are some other options:

  • "Hey NAME, totally understand how busy things can get. Just wanted to give this a gentle nudge in case it got buried. 😊"
  • "Hey NAME, I know the inbox can get crazy! Just wanted to bring this back to the top for you. Hope all’s well!"
  • "Hi NAME! Just following up in case this slipped through the cracks—know how hectic things can get. 😊"
  • "Hey NAME, here is a case study just in case this helps showcase what I can do for you."
  • "Hey NAME! I know things can get busy, so I’m just bumping this up in case it got overlooked. Hope to hear from you soon!"

You can follow up with a prospect more than once, up to three times over as many days with variations of the above follow up messge.

As soon as you get a positive response, arrange the call, make that sale, and get them their transformation!

If you sent the booking link but they didn’t book:

Hey NAME, just wanted to circle back. Did you get a chance to complete your booking?

If they say they booked, but they didn't book.

Hey NAME, just wanted to circle back as I noticed your booking didn't go through. Can I help you complete the registration?
