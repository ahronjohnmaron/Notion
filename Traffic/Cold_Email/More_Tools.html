More Tools

So you’ve got your main tools - email inboxes and cold email sending software. Just using Instantly as a one-stop shop is a great first step and it’s recommended you squeeze all the juice out of there. Eventually at some point, you’ll want to expand into additional tools.

Data Enrichment and Automation

Before we dive into specific software, let’s talk about how a data enrichment and automation platform helps with your cold email efforts.

Think of cold email preparation like making a meal from scratch. You could drive to five different stores - one for produce, one for meat, one for spices, one for sauces, one for bread - load everything into your car each time, transfer it between bags, and hope nothing gets lost in the shuffle.

Or you could go to one well-stocked kitchen that has everything under one roof, where each ingredient flows directly to the next station without you ever leaving the building. Cold email data enrichment and automation software is that kitchen.

What They Do

These tools let you run an entire lead preparation workflow inside a single platform. Instead of downloading a CSV of all your leads, uploading it to one tool for processing, downloading again, and repeating that cycle across four or five different tools, you load your data once and the platform handles the rest in sequence.

A typical workflow might look like this:

  1. Find contact data - locate a prospect’s email address using only their name and company website.
  2. Enrich their profile - pull in additional data points like job title, company size, or LinkedIn activity through web research
  3. Verify their email - confirm the address is valid before you ever hit send.
  4. Personalize the outreach - generate a tailored line or opener using AI.
  5. Push directly to your cold email sending software - no downloading, no re-uploading, no spreadsheet chaos.

Waterfall

In addition to the flow of going from one kitchen station to another, the software can also flow through different service providers and tools within the station.

Staying with our kitchen analogy - imagine you’re at Station 1, and your job is to chop the vegetables. You reach for your first knife. It handles the mushrooms and the zucchini just fine. But then you hit a butternut squash, it has thick skin and that same knife just doesn’t have what it takes. So you don’t stop cooking. You reach for a second knife, a heavier blade from a different brand, built for tougher jobs. That one gets through most of what’s left. But there’s still a few stubborn ones, so you grab a third tool, which is a completely different style, different brand, but has the same goal of cutting the vegetable and it finishes the job.

That’s exactly how waterfall works. When the platform tries to find or verify a piece of data, it doesn’t rely on a single provider. It cascades through a sequence of them - Provider 1, Provider 2, Provider 3 - each one a different source, a different database, a different set of strengths.

Some providers are great at finding emails for well-known companies but come up empty on smaller businesses. Others specialize in exactly those harder-to-find contacts. The platform tries each one in order, and the moment one returns a valid result, it stops and moves on. If none of them can find it, it flags the record and keeps the rest of your list moving. No wasted effort, no stalled workflow. It’s just the best possible result pulled from whatever source can deliver it.

The Tools We Recommend

The most well-known waterfall enrichment platform on the market right now is Clay (clay.com). It’s powerful, flexible, and widely used by serious cold email operators. That said, it can be on the pricier side for people just getting started, which is why we also recommend Bitscale as a strong alternative that delivers similar functionality at a more accessible price point.

Bring Your Own Keys (BYOK)

Here’s something important to understand about how these platforms work and how to use them more cost-effectively.

Enrichment tools run on credits. Each action you take - a verification, an enrichment lookup, an AI-generated line - typically consumes credits, and those credits cost money. When you use a tool’s built-in integrations, you’re essentially paying the platform’s markup on top of the underlying service.

But most platforms, including Bitscale, let you connect your own API keys from third-party providers. Think of it like BYOB, except instead of bringing your own bottle, you’re bringing your own keys.

Here’s a hypothetical example: running email verification through Bitscale’s native credits may cost you one cent per verification. But if you create your own account with MillionVerifier and plug your API key directly into Bitscale, you can run that same verification for let’s say half a cent.

The general principle: use the enrichment platform for the orchestration, the workflow logic, and the tasks that require its native intelligence. For commodity services like email verification, go directly to the source and bring your own key.

Services Worth Setting Up

As you build out your enrichment stack, here are the core services to have in place (connect via API into your Bitscale or Clay):

  1. MillionVerifier for email verification
  2. LeadMagic is great for finding emails.
  3. OpenAI for generating custom openers or tailored lines.
  4. Perplexity for research.

The beauty of having all of this connected inside one enrichment platform is that by the time a lead reaches your sending software, it’s already been found, verified, enriched, and personalized. This is all done within one workflow container in sequence.
