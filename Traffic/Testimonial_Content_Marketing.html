We all start out hoping and believing for testimonials. Then we get them and don't always efficiently share them, or don’t have a system and process to market them effectively.

We use testimonials to:

  1. Build brand credibility
  2. Build belief and trust for clients considering working with you
  3. Build social proof campaigns for conversion marketing
  4. Build the belief of others within the community
  5. Build case studies for you or your sales team to pull from.
  6. Build a feedback loop
  7. Build your belief in the work you’re doing to inspire greater action

Questions to Answer in this training:

1. What is your current process for documenting your client success stories and integrating these stories into your current marketing plan?

2. What client wins are worthy of sharing?

These can be any testimonials that answer potential client objections, show your audience what’s possible, showcase community, edify the tools and support within your community, getting your audience to see themselves in the stories of others.

3. How do you organize, store, and use your testimonials to build your brand, integrate them into your marketing plan and attract new clients for your events and programs?

Testimonial Type, Storing & Organizing

A. Written: Text messages, emails, telegram, or DM’s you receive

B. Video: Cut and Clipped from Screen recordings, clips from your events, mobile video submissions, client spotlights

Storage: Telegram Channel, Zoom → Vimeo

Cutting, Clipping & Designing: Find your “who” in your community who can help.

Understand the Psychology of Testimonial Consumption

Fitness & Weight Loss Testimonials are “Socially Acceptable”

Money, Wealth and Income results: They can create envy, skepticism, judgement of ego, self comparison of someone who is struggling.

Note for making money or wealth niches:

  • Make sure your results are Achievable, Relatable, Human
  • Tie the financial result back to values, such as freedom, confidence, security. Etc
  • Tying it to a free resource in the CTA to invite people into the conversation.
  • Start a separate brand page if you have many.

Hide client identity for unannounced successes to protect community and personal privacy.
