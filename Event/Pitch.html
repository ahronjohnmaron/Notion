Pitch

The pitch is the presentation of your offer to your attendees at the live event. Imagine yourself at a baseball game and you're the pitcher (live event host/speaker) who is pitching (presenting) the baseball (your offer) to the batter (your live event attendee).

Part 1 - Today and the Future

Paint the picture of what the good, so-so, and bad future will look like.

If you look at the gap between what you put in the green versus what you put in the red, this is what it costs to not fulfill your dream. This is the gap. No one plans to be here (circle the red). The reason they end up here is because of a lack of things like community, accountability, mentorship, and strategy. So what I want to share with you is how I can help you reach this goal (circle the green) faster. A lot of people are coming into our community and [accomplishing the things in the green area] and are brand new in coaching and doing this within 90 days and 6 months with us. They were able to do this because we have the tools to help them get there faster. Can I share that with you? Is that ok?

Part 2 - Model

Go through the model (the sides of your triangle) and the 3 items on each side. Play the red, yellow, and green game so the attendees can rate themselves on how they do in each of the 9 areas. If you're using a slideshow or are limited to drawing in only black & white, you can have them do 1-10 to rate themselves instead of using colors. So when you're getting a pulse of where the average is for everyone, you estimate an average number based on their response.

When you're talking about your model, yes it's telling them how you take your clients through transformation, but you're more so framing it as an exercise to help them assess where they are in each of these important pillars and milestones and identifying the gap on where they should be or can go.

How do you help identify the gap? By giving examples of what it looks like if that milestone is bad (red or 1 score), ok (yellow or 5 score), and good (green or 10 score). For example, if your milestone is CEO Mindset, you give an example of what someone with a bad CEO Mindset looks like (they think about how they need to do everything themselves, focused on costs, etc.), ok CEO Mindset (understand they need to hire but don't know how they can afford it, starts work at 9 AM like a job, etc.), and good CEO Mindset (recruiting people who believe in their vision and work for sweat equity, puts 2 hours of work in from 6-8 AM before they start their job at 9 AM, etc.)

All sides of the triangle lead towards the result your coaching does for them and that's written inside the middle of the triangle.

Our goal in our community is to help turn all these areas green. That's what we focus on in our program and that's what we train for. So the way we do this when you get started with us is that we put together a custom game plan for you.

Part 3 - Game Plan

Draw a tic tac toe grid to symbolize the hub and check off different sections of it to illustrate that we have a hub filled with content to cover whatever you need in your custom game plan based on where you are on your path.

As far as the pace goes, we have high expectations for you in terms of your results.

Part 4 - Runway/Roadmap

Go through what milestones your client will accomplish over the term of your program. Example - Month 1 $10k revenue, Month 3 $25k revenue, etc.

As far as our support goes, how do we support you? Keep in mind you can't buy anything today btw. I'm just sharing with you what our program entails, but you can't get started today. We're just going over a quick highlight of how we can support you.

Part 5 - Tools & Support

List all the ways you support your customer so they don't feel like they're thrown into the deep end after the sale without any help. Example - 24/7 Telegram chat group, Facebook group 24-48 hours turnaround time, live training every week with Q&A at the end, coworking hours, quarterly 3-day training event, hub recordings that your entire team gets access, tech vault, 911 emergency email support, networking with other members in the group, swipe files.

Now let's go over pricing because I'm sure everyone is wondering. What does it cost to be a part of this, Adam? Is it ok if I share that? Does anyone want to know how much it costs? One gazillion dollars! Just lmk in the chat if you want to know... Ok great let's go over it.

Part 6 - Pricing

It's a 6-month love or leave it. If you want to get started with us and pay in full, it's $12,997. You probably need 2-3 clients to make that back, but we're not here to just get you 2-3 clients. We're talking about reaching those goals and dreams (in the green).
If you want to do a payment plan, it's $5k down and $1,850 for 5 months.
In order to renew, you have to make your investment back because it wouldn't make sense to stay in a program that doesn't get you results. If you renew, it's $1,200/mo for the next 6 months. Then after that, it's month-to-month.

Drawing

When it comes to pitching your offer, doing it by drawing and talking is very interactive and sucks you into the words as they are spoken and drawn on the screen. Kinda goes back to the old hypnotic text VSL days.

You can use pretty pre-made slides and graphics, but you won't get the same effect as drawing and doing it in real-time. It also seems less salesy because it looks like you're just impromptu talking about what you do and drawing off the cuff (even though you are calculated), as opposed to having a pre-meditated sales pitch using graphics made ahead of time.

You can do your pitch either way, but not having to create slides and graphics will save yourself some time and energy as well. Having graphics and slides though do allow you to not have to remember as much and you can read off the screen as well as having it be clearer than your handwriting. Though in the long run for reasons stated above (maybe after you get a few virtual events under your belt), it's beneficial to do your pitch by drawing it out.

If you are taking the drawing path and you have an iPad and Mac computer or laptop, one solution is to connect your iPad onto Zoom using Airplay to mirror your iPad screen so you can draw and pitch.
