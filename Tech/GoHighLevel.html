Setup

Some things you’ll want to do sooner than later on your HighLevel (HL) setup includes:

1.

Getting your custom domain added and applied to your funnel. If you’re new to HL, here is some instructions on how to use the landing page builder to make edits to your pages.

2.

This is a video on the basic setup for email. And at some point, you’ll want to dive deeper on how to set it up for maximum deliverability.

3.

Buying a phone number you can text through and getting it approved with A2P verification and be aware of daily texting limits as of 2/1/24.

a. For example, if you are sending 4 text reminders per guest on the day of the virtual event, you will hit the daily limit with just 25 attendees, so be ready with your phone, Google Voice, another texting app, to supplement any necessary text messages if you don’t get the limit increased from the starting 100 to the next tier of 250 per day.
b. The way to increase your daily limit is to hit your limit, wait 24 hours (no text messages can be sent during this time), and it’ll resume the next day at the next level. Some HL users in their Facebook group have reported having luck taking to the right person in support to have it increased.
c. For this reason, you may want to pick a Saturday or Sunday where you don’t anticipate text messages being sent from your system and use an automation to trigger a bunch of text messages to yourself to purposely hit the limit so that you start Monday with the next higher limit and it won’t interfere with your event reminders.
d. This ramp up period also needs to be completed before you can change the unsubscribe message and automations. Especially if you are doing database reactivation (texting a bunch of old leads in the database who haven’t heard from you ever or in a long time), it’s recommended to change the unsubscribe message to a keyword that doesn’t red flag your account in Twilio. For example, adding a message that says “Reply DELETE if you don’t want to receive these messages anymore.” and create an automation trigger whenever an SMS is received with that word, it will put the user on DND for SMS.
4.

Setting up WhatsApp in HL for international people instead of texting might be a good idea to increase replies because recipients might not have the ability in their phone plan or want to pay for replying to an international text message. Whereas Whatsapp is free.

5.

Connect your Google Calendar or Microsoft Outlook Calendar, connect your Zoom account, and create your Event Registration and Event Feedback calendars in HighLevel. When setting up these two calendars, a couple of settings that is good to have on is for the meeting location to be using Zoom, and allow Google calendar to send invites. Then for reminders, you can either use the simple ones here or do more advanced ones with different emails and SMS by creating a workflow automation.

Creating Custom Fields

Here is the list of custom fields to create:

1.

Virtual Event Date - Choose date picker. This field will be used for the date of the virtual event your guest will be attending.

2.

Virtual Event Link - Choose single line text. This field will be populated through automations the Zoom link for a specific virtual event date.

3.

Virtual Event Journey - Choose dropdown (single). This field will be used to show the status of where the prospect is as they make their way through your virtual event funnel. Here are the values:

a. Registered, But Did Not Book Interview - When someone registers and opts in for your event, your automation will create the contact in HL with this as the first stage in their journey.
b. Registered, Interview Booked - When someone books their interview call, that appointment booked should trigger an automation to change it to this value.
c. Not Approved to Attend Event - When your interviewer is doing their call with the prospect and determines that they’re not a good fit, manually changing them to this value is a good way to 1) confirm that they attended the call and 2) possibly trigger an automation where you give them some “thank you for showing up but we’re not a good fit so here’s a parting gift for you”. We offer a free mini course (something old we made a while ago) as our way to give them something to soften the “rejection” and feel good about our call nonetheless.
d. Approved to Attend Event - When your interviewer adds the Virtual Event Date, your automation will change the Virtual Event Journey to this field.
e. Attended Event - Your Zoom room manager should be monitoring the wait room and making a list of all the people who attended. Once that fury of attendance is over, they can then go back to the contacts, find their record, and change the value from Approved to Attend Event to Attended Event.
f. Attended Enrollment Call - Similar to the above, your enroller will manually change the field in the contact to Attended Enrollment Call when they have done so.
g. Customer - And once they become a customer, you change the value to this. Or you can create an automation that does so if there is some trigger you’re using for new customers. (You’ll learn more about how automations work as you go through this.)
4.

Interview Followup Date - Choose date picker. This is a date the interviewer can enter if it is determined during the interview that the prospect needs to be followed up with at a later point and not quite ready for a virtual event (or anything that you’re offering right now).

5.

Enrollment Followup Date - Choose date picker. Similar to the above, this is a date the enroller can enter after doing an enrollment call with the prospect, they weren’t ready to buy yet, and needs to be followed up with on a certain date.

Automation Virtual Event - Part 1

Some notes from the above video:

1.

We’re connecting HighLevel to Zapier because we want to get your event registrant’s email added/invited to a Google Calendar event for your virtual event so that they can have it automatically added to their calendar instead of relying on them to manually input or click on any links to add it.

For example, if we were on the phone and I said to you “let’s do lunch on Friday at 12 PM,” you agreed, and I just hung up the phone, you might forget to put it into your calendar and forget your lunch appointment completely. The next best thing would be “ok cool. I just sent you a link to add our lunch into your calendar. Can you click on it so that it gets added?” But the best thing that I can do would be “Great, I just created an event in my Google Calendar and added you to it, so you should get an automated email and it’ll be on your calendar as well. You don’t have to do anything.” Obviously that last option is the best for both logistics and it’s also the least amount of work for you.

This is the same logic for your virtual event for best attendance. However, you’re not going to sit there and manually add every registrant’s email into your Google Calendar event. Instead, you want this automation to do it for you. It requires a little extra technical work, but is part of what helps increase your event show up rate.

Automation Settings

Be sure to allow re-entry in your workflow settings so that contacts can re-enter the automation whenever necessary. For example, someone could have been approved for your event and ready to attend and they’re in your automation for reminders, but they had to cancel and reschedule for a future event. By allowing re-entry, they can re-enter the automation for the reminders (but for a different date).

Opportunities

Concurrently with the custom field, Virtual Event Journey, you may want to add into your automations to create a new opportunity at the point of approved to attend (when you add the virtual event date).

This will allow you and your sales team to use the pipeline as a way to:

  • •Visually track the journey and see where the fallout is occurring from approved to attend, attended the event, attended enrollment call, and customer.
  • •Tally up opportunity/lead value at each stage of the event for revenue projections.

And of course as the change stages in the pipeline, have an automation that changes the custom field stage as well.
