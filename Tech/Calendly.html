Creating a New Account

If you are doing the interview calls and enrollment calls yourself, just one account is required under your email. Otherwise, you will want a separate account for each person made with an email under your own domain.

•
You do NOT want to make the account under YOUR email and give them access - If you do this, all the email notifications about bookings will go to you and onto your calendar and not theirs.
•
You do NOT want to make the account under a free Gmail account and give them access - If you do this, they can always change the password and attach text verification to their phone and you will never be able to access the email/account again.
•
You do NOT want to use the interviewer or enrollment specialist's personal email account or their Calendly account if they have one - If you do this, all the time you spend setting up the events and workflows in their account will go with them when they eventually leave the position and you will have to set them up all over again for the next person. Also, you will need their password to log in and make changes to the account, which can be awkward if it's their account.

What you DO want is to create an account for each interviewer and enrollment specialist (if they are the same person, then one account is fine) with a new email address under your business domain like event@yourbusiness.com. The reason you want to make it under your domain is that you have the administrative permissions to reset the password to the email account anytime you need to (for hiring and firing) and not get locked out of the calendar account.

You’ll also do NOT want to use SSO login with your Google or Microsoft account because you may need an assistant or tech support or someone to log into your account or theirs and that would require giving your password to your email, which isn’t very secure thing to do, and often times these email accounts have additional security measures like text verification and further complicates access. Instead, you’ll want to use just an email and password login method. If you do have it under SSO login, you can unlink your Google/Microsoft account.

Once you've got your Calendly account created, you'll eventually need a Standard account ~$12/mo because you'll want to use their automated text message reminders feature.

Account Settings

Profile - Configure as much as you can on this page with the exception of the Welcome Message because that's not relevant to what we're doing with live events.

Branding - Turn off Calendly Branding and add your logo if available.

Phone Number - Add your mobile phone number so that you can receive a text reminder for upcoming calls.

Integrations

Calendar Integration - You definitely want to connect your calendar to Calendly (typically Google or Microsoft) because this is how it's able to create a calendar event in your calendar and add your prospect's email as an invitee so that it shows up in their calendar. Integrating your calendar is also important for Calendly to see when you're busy and unavailable for a call so that you can't be booked during that time if you have a doctor's appointment for example.

Email Integration - You can connect your email so that emails come from your email/domain instead of Calendly, but it's recommended NOT to due to deliverability. Emails sent from Calendly tend to have a less likely chance of going into the spam folder than emails sent from your email through Calendly. Either way, if they reply to your email, it will go to the email you log into your Calendly account with.

ActiveCampaign - If you are using this for your email, you can integrate it so that everyone who is booked for an interview, event, and/or feedback call can be tagged appropriately for email list segmentation purposes and campaigns.

Zoom Integration - The benefit of integrating Zoom into Calendly is that recordings are neatly labeled for each interview or enrollment call; otherwise the recordings are labeled by timestamp. However, keep in mind that sometimes there are bugs in the integration and their static Zoom Personal Meeting ID link will be used instead. Nonetheless, do the Zoom integration.

Setting Up Pre-event Interview Call

If you have more than one interviewer and doing a round robin, then take the following steps before creating the event:

1.
Under the admin/main account in the Admin Management section, invite all the users who will be interviewers to the account. You do NOT need to create a group during this process. Just invite and add all the users to the account.
2.
Next, create a team, call it Interviewers, and add all the interviewers as Team Members. If you are adding or removing members to your interview team, go to your interview team page from the homepage and then click on the gear icon to edit the team.
3.
After the team has been created, it will prompt you to create your first event and choose Round Robin. You can then proceed with the instructions below.

If you have just one person doing all the interviews, you Create New Event Type - Choose One-on-One.

Event Name - Call it something like [EVENT NAME] Confirmation because it will be a quick "confirmation call." Some entrepreneurs call it [EVENT NAME] Alignment Call. What you don't want to do is call it and Interview Call because it can be intimidating to attendees.

Location - Choose Zoom as the location and it'll create a unique Zoom link for the event. That's great for security, but the real benefit is that the cloud autorecordings in Zoom will be neatly labeled by the event name and person versus a generic timestamp if you had use the static Zoom Personal Meeting ID link. (If you are doing a round robin and setting the location and distribution, you'll also want to likely set it to Optimize for Availability rather than for Equal Distribution. Their documentation explains the difference here.)

Click Next to proceed to the next screen

Date range - Invitees can schedule... - You have a couple of options here:

1.
One method is to choose Within a date range option and select dates (as early as you'd like to do interviews) all the way up to a day or two before the event (whenever you want to stop doing interviews).
2.
Other option is to choose 3-4 calendar days into the future so that guests are limited to booking an interview that 3-4 days instead of many days later when they might forget or lose interest and are less likely to show up. If you choose this option, be sure to later set custom hours and add date overrides so that the window of interviews stay within a certain range, such as today through the day of the event.

Duration - 15 minutes is recommended for the time slot with the intention of keeping the call down to 10-12 minutes so that you have a few minutes before your next back-to-back call. Keep in mind you can extend it slightly longer to 20 minutes if you'd like, but the problem is the longer the duration, the less interview calls you can do per day.

How do you want to offer your availability for this event type? - The option is up to you, but the most common is the "Use an existing schedule" option.

Additional rules for your availability > Start time increments - If you've set 15 minutes as the duration, you will want increments of 15 minutes. If you set 20 minutes for your interview duration, make the increments 20 minutes. Doing this maximizes the number of calls per hour.
Scheduling conditions > Invitees can't schedule within... - By default it's 4 hours, but you may want to shorten it depending on what you're comfortable with. That means if it's 12 PM right now, the earliest someone can book with you is 4 PM. If you reduce it to 2 hours, then it's 2 PM. The purpose of this is to give yourself enough warning before a call comes up.

Click Next to proceed to the next screen

Additional Options - Invitee Questions - By default, there is an option to Add Guest, so you'll want to click on the pencil and check off "Autofill Invitee Name, Email, and Text Reminder Phone Number (when applicable) from prior bookings" and uncheck "Allow invitees to add additional guests" so that it looks like this. You'll then want to click on the pencil to modify the "Please share anything that will help prepare for our meeting" and change it to Mobile and required Phone Number field.

When your prospect loads the Calendly booking, you will see two mobile fields - the one you just made here called "Mobile" and one provided by Calendly called "Send text messages to". The reason we add this custom required field Mobile is because we can't force users to input their mobile into Calendly's "Send text messages to" field for text reminders. So what would happen is that most prospects would skip out on inputting their mobile and we wouldn't be able to call or text them for reminders or follow up if it's a no show. Thus, we need to add a required Mobile field that forces them to give us their mobile so we have it for manual follow up and hopefully that will encourage them to input their mobile into the "Send text messages to" field as well so that they receive the automated Calendly text reminders that you'll be creating later in the workflows.

Notifications and Cancellation Policy > Email confirmations - You will want to revise the Subject and Body so that it looks something like this.

Show Cancellation Policy > Uncheck "Include cancel and reschedule links in notifications" - Doing so makes it inconvenient for prospects to cancel so that they're more likely to stick to the appointment. Then click the save and close button.

Then click on the share button and add to website to get the code you need to embed your calendar into the booking page.

You're all set with setting up the interview call as an event, so be sure to see below later for Pre-event Interview Call Reminders - AUTOMATED - If you're booking interviews through Calendly, be sure to input these email and text reminders as workflows. Even though these are automated reminders, it's always a good idea to have your interviewer manually text and confirm all their appointments in the morning for their entire day.

Setting Up Live Event

First off, we need to determine which Calendly account you will be setting up the live event under:

•
If you anticipate having 99 or less attendees for the event, make this under your own/main Calendly account.
•
If you anticipate having 100 or more attendees for the event, you will need to create the live event in each of your enroller's Calendly accounts or have a second Calendly event/account.
•
Whichever Calendly account you're using for the live event, be sure to go to the Calendar Connections, click on the edit button in the Add to calendar section, and uncheck the Deleting or declining an event in your calendar will also cancel it in Calendly and click update.
•
Some people have asked if the guest's information is hidden when using Calendly for your live event. When we tested it, we used our SBM Google Calendar connected into Calendly and a personal Gmail/Google user as the test guest. When we did that, the email invite had no other guest's information in it nor did the guest's calendar event. If you go into your calendar that's connected to Calendly though, you will see how many guests are booked and their names. Results may differ if using Microsoft calendar, so best to test yourself using a different/personal email and book that into the Calendly event to see what your guest would see.

Creating Day 1

Create New Event Type - Choose Group.

Event name - Call it [EVENT NAME] Day 1 because you will later be duplicating it to make Day 2. You'll need a group event for each day.

Location - Change this to custom and input the Zoom link to your live event. By doing it this way, you dictate to Calendly which Zoom link to use (versus choosing Zoom as the location and Calendly would make a link for you). Reminder that you'll probably want to use the Zoom Personal Meeting ID (versus generated automatically) as the live event link so that you'll never have to change it when doing future events.

Description/Instructions - If you'd like, you can add a subheadline or anything about your event.

Max invitees in a spot - Set this to 99 because that is the limit for text notifications for a group event.

Display remaining spots - Check off this box because it's a nice way at glance to see how many people have registered. For example, if 99 is the max and there are 79 invites left, then that means there are 20 registrations.

Click Next to proceed to the next screen

Date range - Invitees can schedule... - Choose Within a date range option and double click the date that will be the first date of the event so that the "range" will the same date through the same date (February 6, 2023 in that example) and then click the apply button. (When you duplicate this event later to make Day 2, then you would modify this to be the next date, such as February 7, 2023.)

Duration - Change this to a custom time so you can set it for the duration of your event (typically for 3 hours).

How do you want to offer your availability for this event type? - Choose "Set custom hours," make sure the Time Zone is set for your time zone, and check off all the days of your event and the times. So if it's Monday through Tuesday from 2-5 PM Pacific, it will look like this. (The reason you're setting it for all the days is because when you're duplicating the event for Day 2, you won't have to modify this section and it'll just be the date range.)

Additional rules for your availability > Scheduling conditions - Change this to 0 hours so that your interviewer can book an attendee all the way up to the event last minute if necessary.

Click Next to proceed to the next screen

Additional Options - Invitee Questions - You'll want to have Name and Email, which means you may need to delete anything additional Calendly has inputted by default (like Please share anything that will help prepare for our meeting). You do NOT need to add a field for mobile for text reminders because they will automatically do that when you add a workflow that has a text in it. Then click save and close.

Notifications and Cancellation Policy > Email confirmations - You will want to revise the Subject and Body so that it looks something like this. What you're doing is removing some of the unnecessary variables that Calendly adds by default.

Show Cancellation Policy > Uncheck "Include cancel and reschedule links in notifications" - Doing so makes it inconvenient for prospects to cancel so that they're more likely to stick to the appointment. Then click the save and close button.

Confirmation Page > Display button to schedule another event? - Toggle this to yes to make it convenient for the interviewer to book the next call after they're done with their current one.

View live page - This is the link to your live event Day 1 that your interviewer will need to book the interviewee for the live event during the interview.

Creating Day 2

Now we're going to make Day 2 of your event by cloning the Day 1.

Event name - Rename it by changing the 1 to 2 so that it's Day 2.

Event link - Rename the slug by deleting the "1-clone" part and replacing that with 2.

Click Next to proceed to the next screen

Date range - Invitees can schedule... - Modify the Within a date range option by double clicking the date that will be the second date of the event so that the "range" will the same date through the same date like the first time you did it. Using the example from last time being Feburary 6, 2023 as Day 1 of the event, this time it will be February 7, 2023 because we're modifying it for Day 2. Then click the save and close button and you're done!

View live page - This is the link your live event Day 2 that your interviewer will need to book the interviewee for the live event during the interview.

Turning on the event - Now that you’re done setting up this Day 2 event, you have to turn it on because by default a cloned event is off.

Testing Availability for Both Days

Now test the availability by loading both booking page links for the event and you should see then available for you to book people into each day.

A common reason why it displays no availability is that you may have an event booked into the Google/Microsoft calendar you have connected to Calendly. (You may have manually inputted the event into your own calendar to remind yourself of your live event.) Be sure to delete and clear your calendar so that Calendly sees it as free and available so that you can book people in for your event. Once you do the first person (you can test it with a personal Gmail email later after you’ve inputted the workflows so you can see the email you get), the event should be created on your Google/Microsoft calendar.

Setting Up Enrollment Call Event

Create New Event Type - Choose One-on-One

Event Name - Call it [EVENT NAME] Feedback Call because that is what it is to the attendee (one of the conditions for attending the event for free was so that we could get their "feedback" after the event). Do NOT call it an Enrollment Call (or Sales Call) because that is for internal reference only. Some people frame them as a Celebration Call, so adjust accordingly.

Location - Choose Zoom as the location and it'll create a unique Zoom link for the event. That's great for security, but the real benefit is that the cloud autorecordings in Zoom will be neatly labeled by the event name and person versus a generic timestamp if you had use the static Zoom Personal Meeting ID link.

Description/Instructions - Enter something like: I'm super excited to get your feedback and hear what you thought about the event, what you learned, and answer any questions you might have!

Click Next to proceed to the next screen

Date range - Invitees can schedule... - Choose Indefinitely into the future option because then you can use this indefinitely. However, it's best practice for the enrollment call to be booked as soon as possible after the event.

Duration - 30 minutes. Keep in mind you can extend it slightly longer to 45 in case people are late or you think you'll need more time, but the problem is the longer the duration, the less enrollment calls you can do per day.

How do you want to offer your availability for this event type? - This will depend on the enroller, but typically it's easiest using the existing schedule and blocking out any times the enroller is busy on their calendar directly (like a lunch break for example).
Want to add time before or after your events? This is where you can add buffer before and after the event. It's recommended to keep this to 0 and adjust the event duration instead.

Additional rules for your availability > Start time increments - Change this to 5 min for reduce any unnecessary downtime in between appointments. However, if your feedback calls are 30 minutes long (with zero buffer), set this to be every 30 minutes. If it's 45 minutes long, you can set this to every 15 minutes.

Workflows

Next, we'll create the workflows, which are automated email and text messages for your live event and enrollment calls, and apply them to the events we just created. Here are all the Calendly templates to input:

Pre-event Interview Call Reminders - AUTOMATED - If you're booking interviews through Calendly, be sure to input these email and text reminders as workflows. Even though these are automated reminders, it's always a good idea to have your interviewer manually text and confirm all their appointments in the morning for their entire day.

Pre-event Interview Call Completed and Approved to Attend Event - After confirming over Zoom that the attendee is a good fit for your event and they are approved for registration, the interviewer sends them this welcome email with the Zoom link and rest of the event details. This can be automated as a workflow email sent to invitee immediately when new event is booked (for event type, choose just Day 1 of your live event).

Live Event Reminders - These countdown marketing emails and reminders are for letting the approved attendees know that the event is coming up.

💡
Note that when you’re adding these reminders, you’re using the Calendly Location variable and not the actual Zoom link for the event because Calendly will automatically pull the location (the Zoom link you have in the event) so that you don’t have to change the Zoom link in the emails when you do the next event.

Post-event Enrollment Call Reminders - AUTOMATED - These email and text messages are to remind the event attendee of their upcoming enrollment call.
