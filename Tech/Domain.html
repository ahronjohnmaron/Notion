Domain

The very first step is deciding if you need a separate domain or will be using your existing one. Often times it's recommended that you get a separate domain, but here are some things to consider when deciding.

Remember, you’re also getting a domain for your event or brand and not your offer/program.

Branding

For example, if you are Grant Cardone and you just launched your new book The 10X Rule and want to have a live event around it, getting a domain like 10xevent.com is punchier, shorter, and more relevant than working off an existing main domain GrantCardone.com/10xevent. By the way, that doesn't mean you can't have that section on your main site, you just make it redirect to the 10xevent.com website.

Ease of Remembering and Typing

If you already have a main domain that is kind of long like GrantCardoneTherapist.com because Grant Cardone (the entrepreneur) owns GrantCardone.com, you'll probably want a new domain for your live event; otherwise, you'll have to promote GrantCardoneTherapist.com/event. Why is the length of your domain important? When it comes to promoting it, there will be times you have to verbally say the domain and people will have to remember it and type it. If it's a super long, it's harder to remember and type (especially on a smartphone).

Tech Conflict

Perhaps the most important reason on why you'd want to get a new domain is the potential conflict with the tech used on your main site. For example, you may use Wix for your main site because you got it when you just started, had no tech person so you needed something easy to use so that you can do it yourself, etc. But now come time for your live event, getting the pages to technically work on the Wix platform can possibly be difficult. By getting a new domain, you and/or your tech person have maximum flexibility with the tools available. To get a better overall understanding of what we mean, continue reading the rest of this document to understand all the tech required and you can assess whether the platform your current website can support it or not.

Using AI to Write Your Sales Copy

If you plan on using AI like ChatGPT, Copy.ai, Jasper.ai, ChatSonic or similar to write your headline or copy for your event registration page, you definitely want to make sure your event is hosted on a separate domain because Google will penalize the site in its search engine results if it detects AI written content. You do NOT want to risk getting your site penalized if you spent time and money getting it SEO'ed and ranked with your blog and all by having AI-detected written sales copy for your event registration webpage. Since we don't use SEO to get organic traffic to your event registration webpage, it's ok (and highly suggested) to use AI writing tools to help you with all the heavy lifting.
