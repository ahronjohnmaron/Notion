SMS Compliance

Texas

Effective September 1, 2025, Texas Senate Bill 140 (SB 140) expands their telemarketing rules to SMS and requires marketers using SMS to adhere to their guidelines.

This applies to you if you are a businesses located in Texas sending SMS OR sending SMS to residents of Texas.

Since it’s not easy to tell if the person you’re texting is a resident of Texas just based on their area code, it would be best practice if you adhered to these Texas rules, including registration with the Texas Secretary of State (a $200 filing fee) and a $10,000 security deposit (most popular way to satisfy this is getting a bond that costs about $100/yr from a vendor like A1 Surety Bonds).

An exception would be if they are your existing client, but of course, do your research to ensure you have the most updated information. There are plenty of articles online about this law. This attorney wrote a good summary and SOP.

One rule you’ll want to make sure you have in place is that no new outgoing messages (initiated by you and not in response to something they requested like sending them an SMS confirmation after an optin) is that you have your messaging window set (you can set this in HighLevel in your workflows) inside the hours of 9 AM - 9 PM Monday-Saturday and 12-9 PM on Sundays, in Texas local time.
