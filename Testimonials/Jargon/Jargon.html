Jargon

Below is a list of commonly used words and what they mean in online marketing and particularly in the context of Smart Business Mastermind (SBM).

Offer - Your offer is the package of your products and services that you are offering your prospects. It consists of the following major components below:

  1. Model - The model is your philosophy/approach/methodology on how exactly you will help your customers. It's often drawn in a triangle where each of the 3 sides is called a Pillar and each pillar has three Milestones. See Model for more details.
    • When presenting your model during the pitch, you play the red light, yellow light, green light game to help your audience assess where they are for each pillar/milestone. See pitch definition below for more details.
  2. Tools and Support - These are the deliverables of what your customers get when they buy from you and different ways they can get help, such as weekly Zoom calls, Facebook Group, and Telegram (TG). Some other things included in Tools and Support that are to be further defined:
    • Hub or Back Office - This is the password protected area where paid customers can access your training videos and additional content. There are different software that can do this, but Kajabi is one of the most common and recommended.
    • Code Red Support - This is referred to as an emergency last resort type of help for your customer where they can send a private email asking for support.
  3. Pricing - How much your offer costs presented first in Paid in Full (PIF) and then with a payment plan option.

Pitch - The pitch is the presentation of your offer during the live event. See Pitch for more details.

Multi-day Live Virtual Event - This is a long version of saying it, but wanted to type it out this way to illustrate the main components:

  • Multi-day - Meaning more than 1 day. Your event should span over 2 days (and be 3 hours long each). We used to do 3-day long events (and you possibly will see it referenced that way in our content somewhere), but we've shortened it to 2 days because we found that it didn't negatively affect sales. Some thing with the hours. It's 3 hours long and not all-day for 8 hours. We've found that 3 hours for 2 days is the optimal balance of output (your time and energy for preparing and running the event) for results (impact for the event attendees and getting sales afterwards).
  • Live - The word live gives it a distinction that it's not a recorded automated webinar. It's taught live with live audience participation.
  • Virtual - This is to make the distinction that it's done online versus in-person.
  • With all that said as the proper terminology, to keep it simple you'll see us reference it as your: event, live event, virtual event, or live virtual event. In regards to terminology for your prospects, you may want to call it something like online workshop, online conference, or master class because they might not understand any of our industry jargon.

Virtual Summit - This is an internet marketing industry term for an online/virtual event that typically consists of multiple speakers (can be dozens over a span of days). For your SBM event, you'll want to limit your speakers to just you by yourself or with 1 (or 2 max) guest speakers because if any more than that, you risk them taking the spotlight away from you and your offer. But again, we refer to it as an event and not summit.

Live Event Funnel - Now that you know what a live event is, you may see the term live event funnel, which is a specific version of a "funnel". In the internet marketing world, a funnel is a method you're using to funnel prospects into your ecosystem. In the SBM live event funnel, we're using a free live event as a way to attract prospect customers and funnel them into your ecosystem.

Landing Page - A landing page is another name for a webpage, but the reason it’s specifically referred to as a landing page is because it’s the webpage someone "lands on" based on the specific instructions you gave them on what to click or where to go. Calling a webpage a "landing page" means there was intention behind directing a person to that specific webpage versus any other webpage or area of your website. In the context of SBM, there are only 2 landing pages we use (guest pass for warm traffic and event registration for cold traffic). See #4 - Event Registration for more details. Here are some more terms that you'll want to know that often relate to landing pages:

  • Headline - If you think of a landing page like a newspaper, a newspaper will have the most interesting article on the front page with a big and bold headline for that article. It's designed to capture the reader's attention to pick up that newspaper off the stand and buy it. It's the same concept on a landing page. Often times there's also a pre-headline (smaller text that goes before/above the headline) and a sub-headline or tagline (smaller text that goes after/below the headline).
  • Call-to-action (CTA) - The CTA is referring to the specific action you want the reader to take. You are calling them to take action. This can be things like clicking a button/link or entering their contact info into some fields.
  • Optin - The optin typically refers to the section of your landing page where the visitor gives you their name, email, and/or mobile. This is the act of opting in - they are choosing to give you their contact information so that they can access your information and you can market to them.
  • Copy - The dictionary defines this well as the text of an advertisement. In our context specifically, it typically refers to the text on your webpages, but it can also refer to the text in your emails.
  • Success Page - The success page is the next webpage a person successfully sees after they've taken the action you want (typically on your landing page). In our context, if a person lands on your event registration landing page and fills out their contact information to register for the event, the success page is the webpage where they book their interview call. Then the success page to that interview call booking page can be a webpage where it says thank you for successfully booking your interview call and here's what you do next. So in this example, the success page can have a success page too. Similar to how a son of a father can also have a son himself. And generally speaking, the son is the successor to the father. Same with webpages and the concept of success page.
  • Lead magnet - Although this isn't used much in the SBM model, it is something you will want to have for your business at some point. A lead magnet is like a carrot you dangle to your prospect in exchange for their contact information. The carrot is an enticing thing that acts as a magnet to attract leads into your ecosystem. A lead magnet is used to build your email list so you have an effective way to contact everyone (via email) to promote your live event and other products and services. A common example of a lead magnet would be a free PDF ebook.

Pre-event Confirmation Call - This is the secret sauce to having the majority of people who register for your event to actually show up and attend your event with enthusiasm and eagerness to learn. These calls are done BEFORE the event; hence the word pre-event. For short, these calls can be referred to as pre-event calls, event confirmation calls, verbal confirmation calls, or simply confirmation calls. Some people have also framed these calls to their prospects as alignment calls because it's to see if they are aligned with the content, values, etc. of the event. See Pre-event Interview Calls for more details.

  • The above terms are used EXTERNALLY to the prospect. INTERNALLY at SBM and among your team, they can be referred to as pre-event interviews, pre-event interview calls, interviews, or interview calls. You may also see it accidentally referenced as pre-interview calls in our content, but we really meant pre-event interview call. The reason why these are terms used internally is because the word "interview" can possibly be intimidating to your prospect and discourage them from registering because they may be afraid it's a long process, scary, think they'll be rejected and won't get in, etc.
  • Interviewer - This is the name of the role of the person who does these interview calls. You may also see this referenced as Pre-event Specialist or jokingly even as the Gate or Bouncer because they're screening people to let them into the event or not. The person who does the interview calls can be the same or different than the person who does the post-event feedback calls, but keep in mind the person doing the feedback calls will require sales skills whereas interview calls just require people skills.

Post-event Feedback Call - These are the calls done AFTER the event; hence the word post-event. They are called feedback calls because they are framed to the prospect as a call after the event to get their feedback on the event. Some people have also framed it as a Celebration Call.

  • The above terms are used EXTERNALLY to the prospect. INTERNALLY at SBM and among your team, they can be referred to as Enrollment Call or simply as a sales call because the primary intent of the call is to enroll the prospect into your program and make the sale.
  • Enrollment Specialist - This is the name of the role of the person who does these enrollment/sales calls. You may also see this referenced as Post-event Enrollment Specialist or Post-event Specialist.

Price Floor - This is a sales method where you have a floor price (the lowest you're willing to go) on your program and your enrollment specialist is authorized to reduce the price of your program as low as the price floor in order to overcome any price objection and make the sale. The act of doing this can be referenced in its verb form of price flooring.

Scholarship - This a graceful way to frame a discount. Rather than give a discount on your price, you give your prospect a scholarship (like a student would get at a university).

Gap - The gap is a term often used in sales where you help the customer realize where they are now and how far they need to go (the gap) to get to where they want to be. Prospects must realize this gap exists in order to want to buy your product or service because it is the vehicle that will bridge/close that gap. A couple of places in the SBM business model where you use the gap concept is during the Pitch where you talk about Part 1 - Today and the Future and play the red light, yellow light, and green light game during Part 2 - Model.

Upselling - Upselling is immediately selling something after someone has bought something. Hey you just bought this thing, why don't you buy this add-on thing. Upselling is effective because your customer has already whipped out their credit card, entered their details, and bought something, so they're very much open to buying something else next.

Downselling - Conversely to upselling, downselling is selling something (typically of lesser price) after someone has said no to buying the first thing. Let's say on an enrollment call your prospect is pretty adamant about not buying your $5,000 program because they can't attend the live program. At that point, you can downsell into a set of recorded videos you have that are $1,000.

VIP Day or Intensive - These typically refers to a service where you spend a day or half day with your client. It can also be referred to as an intensive because you're giving them focused intense attention and/or helping your client get intense attention and results in their business.

Discovery Call - This is typically a free call you do with a prospect to discover what needs they have so that you can present them a solution (typically a product or service you offer). This can also be referred to as a Strategy Call, Consultation, or Consulting Call. Often times these are free get-to-know-you assessment calls and then a second call is arranged to provide the solution and where the sales take place (though they can take place during the first call as well). We've seen these second calls framed as a Roadmap Call.

  • In the context of SBM, events are a way to sell one-to-many. You're doing a sales presentation (the pitch) once to many people during the event. Discovery calls are one-to-one sales where you're doing a sales presentation individually. Thus, we don't do discovery calls often.
  • The primary exceptions though are 1) the very beginning where you haven't launched your first event yet and you want/need to make sales right away, you'll do these types of calls and use our 7-step Sales Framework.
  • And 2) in between events where you don't want a prospect to wait until the next event comes around or they aren't able to attend.

Done For You (DFY) - Done for you is a way to describe a service you provide where you do it for your client. In our context, we have SBM where we show you how to build a live event funnel and you Do It Yourself (DIY). We also have Dream Factory, an DFY online marketing agency, that builds the live event funnel for you. Done With You (DWY) also exists where it's some combination. Understanding DIY, DWY, and DFY is useful for constructing your offer and business model.

Onboarding - This is typically referring to the first meeting you have with your new paid customer who signed up with you to help them get onboarded/acclimated with your program.

Evergreen - This word is commonly used in internet marketing to refer to content that is timeless. There isn't anything in the content that could be outdated. For example, an article that talks about leadership is timeless because leadership will never expire. Whereas an article on how to market on Black Friday 2022 isn't evergreen because it doesn't apply year round and the tactics could expire in 2023 and beyond. In SBM context, evergreen can be used in when it comes to enrollment or your program. That means instead of having your program be like semesters in college where there's a spring semester/class and winter semester/class with a definitive start and end date so there's only a limited window a student can enroll into class, you have an evergreen enrollment or program where your customers can enroll and sign up at any time. An evergreen business model allows you to sign up clients regularly and have a more consistent cash flow type of business instead of an ebb and flow of having a business model where your 6-month coaching program is only open for enrollment (and making sales) January 15-31 because it starts on February 1 for example.

Direct Message (DM) - This is often referred to as DM marketing or DMing, which is a marketing strategy we use at SBM to reach out to prospects on Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn for example by sending them a direct message (or private message or mail) into their inbox.

Speak and Sweep - This is referring to the strategy of doing an Instagram Live interview with someone who is a prospect for your business and/or has your target audience. See Clubhouse & IG Live Conversions Pt 2: IG Live (Speak & Sweep) for more details.

Taprooting - This is a bring a friend strategy to increase attendees to your live event. You can go through your own list of contacts and ask them if they have a friend who can benefit from your workshop and have them introduce you to them or see if the friend can invite them to your event. You can also do the same with the people who are registered to attend your event.

Virtual Assistant (VA) - A VA is an assistant who works for you from their home. In SBM, a VA is recommended as one of your first hires and to have them do DM marketing for you. If you're paying your VA directly and they are in another country, you'll likely need to complete a W-8BEN form, but of course consult your CPA. You can avoid this by using an intermediary such as Upwork.

Event Tracker - This is an example spreadsheet you can use for your interviews, live events, and enrollment calls to track important information and metrics. Link to Event Tracker here.

Show-up Rate or Attendance Rate - This is the rate that someone shows up or attends your interview call, live event, and/or enrollment call. See the Conversions tab on the Event Tracker for more details.

Edification Circle - This is the part of an SBM live event (and we recommend it for your event too) where we edify the attendees. We are giving them compliments, saying something positive, and acknowledging them. This is a fantastic exercise to raise the vibration, energy, connectedness, and more towards the end of an event. You can see an example of this being done in Day 3: Mutual Encouragement & Closing.

Playbook - This is a term we use to refer to a set of strategies written down that you can refer to and follow. Consider it like an instruction manual for an aspect of your business. We have ours and we encourage you to build your own because it is an important part of growing and scaling a business.

JV Partnership - JV stands for joint venture and JV partnerships is about building relationships with people who will help you promote and market your event and products and services. You may also see these people referred to as Affiliates. See Leveraging Your JV Partnerships for more details.
