Testimonials

A fantastic habit/system to create in your business is to ask for and gather testimonials because social proof is a powerful motivator to encourage more people to work with you. When a prospect sees someone (or a lot of people) who was in the same position as them and the results you were able to help them achieve, they become much more inclined to work with you. Thus, you should be obtaining testimonials as often as you can.

Proactively Getting Testimonials

One way to obtain testimonials is to go out and get them. This means taking proactive opportunities to ask people for testimonials (whether you have a few minutes or several minutes). In the context of our business, here are some opportune times to get testimonials:

  • During an enrollment call, you should be able to elicit some positive comments about their experience of your virtual event so that you can use it on your event registration page and other marketing material when encouraging other people in the future to sign up for your next event.
  • Immediately after a client has a big win with your program. Note that it’s very difficult to get someone to just make a testimonial for you. It’s like giving them homework. It’s another thing they need to do. Instead, you want to make it easy for them by asking when they can do a quick 10-minute Zoom call so that you can learn more about their win and experience. In some instances you’ll be able to let them know it’ll include a testimonial so that they know to be camera ready for that call.
  • During your regular/weekly trainings at the beginning before you start, you can ask who had some wins this week (your trainings should be auto-recorded).
  • On a break or after a VIP Day or a coaching session.

During these moments, the best case is that you’ll be able to video edit the recordings and use it as a video testimonial. If they are uncomfortable with that, you can pull quote snippets from the transcript and create a written testimonial with a photo of them.

Testimonial Questions to Ask

Ok so now you’ve got someone who is ready to give you some wonderful words about you and your program. How do you ask them for the testimonial? Here is a list of questions for inspiration or choose the ones you like (you don’t have to necessarily ask all of them):

  1. What was your life and business like before you met us?
  2. What was a specific, concrete problem you were facing in your life or business?
  3. What were some of your concerns before working together? What hesitations or fears did you have before you actually signed up with us?
  4. How did we help you specifically?
  5. What are the results you’ve had in your life since working with us?
  6. What about your business? What results have you had there? Over what time frame?
  7. What are some other areas of your life that have been affected as a result?
  8. How have others in your life benefitted from the transformations you’ve made in your life and business? Tell us more about that.
  9. What did you take away that you weren’t expecting?
  10. What are you confident in being able to apply just from what you’ve learned so far?
  11. What would be the cost of not working with us?
  12. What do you see possible because of what you learned today?
  13. What would you say to someone who is considering working with us?
  14. Anything else you want the people at home to know?

Here is a great list of non-results focused questions from @gentlebusinesscoach

  1. What was your favorite part about being supported by me?
  2. What has been one of the most powerful things you’ve learned or integrated from our work together?
  3. If a friend asked you to describe what it’s like to be supported by me, what would you say?
  4. If you were to recommend my work to someone, what would you say?
  5. What could have been done to make you feel even more supported, cared for, or held?
✅

Pro Tip #1 - Let them know not to worry about being perfect in saying everything smoothly all at once (they can answer multiple times) because your video editor can do jump cuts and edit out long silences, moments when they’re thinking, outtakes, etc.

✅

Pro Tip #2 - When they’re answering the question, have them repeat the question in the answer so that it’s a complete sound bite. Example question: What is your favorite color? They’d respond with: My favorite color is green.

Collecting Testimonials That Come In

What’s great is that people will give you testimonials and say nice things about you and your service here and there. When they do, be sure to capture it!

These can be messages and comments in social media, instant chat messages, or emails. Basically anytime they say something nice, you need a method of collecting and adding them to your collection (ideally in an organized database).

One of the easiest ways to do that is by taking screenshots of the testimonial and emailing them or if it’s an email itself, you forward the email. If you’re using something like Gmail, you can send those emails to yourself or someone on your team by adding the + sign (so for example we can send the emails to support+testimonial@smartbusinessmastermind.com) and create a Gmail filter that automatically labels and archives incoming emails to that address with “testimonial” so that once a week or month you can process them for marketing.

What does process mean? Ideally it’s adding them to your testimonial database where you have each testimonial tagged based on whatever characteristics that are important to your business, such as a particular niche, a certain result, or a certain situation. (You may also need to redact it [like their name] for anonymity.)

For example with us, we can tag client testimonials based on whether they’re a coach in the fitness niche, faith based niche, or whatever niche so that whenever a fitness prospect is interested in our program, we can pull up all the fitness testimonials relevant to their situation.
